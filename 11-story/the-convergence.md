# The Convergence

> ⚠️ **GM-only.** The campaign frame: every clock the vault has set running, laid
> on one table. The source bible gave the setting one far clock (the Shriven,
> ~200 years). The expansion waves have added near ones. They cluster.

## The clocks

| Clock | Reads | Zero |
|---|---|---|
| The Cabal's **Hollow Key** | "approaching critical experiments" (canon) | any time now |
| The [Imperial succession](../09-power-and-conflict/politics/imperial-succession.md) | the Emperor's heart is failing; Kythera said *"Not yet. You'll understand why."* | ~1–3 years |
| The [22nd Centenary Conclave](../09-power-and-conflict/politics/divine-politics.md) | the Twelve convene at [Crossroads](../07-geography/crossroads.md) | **2,100 AC — 4 years** |
| The Pale's [Descending Liturgy](../07-geography/pale-interior.md) | ~2,190 and falling daily | **~2,102 AC — 6 years** |
| The [Shriven](../03-hidden-truth/the-shriven.md) | the Great Alignment | ~200 years |

Note the shape: succession → Conclave → the Number, each two-ish years apart —
a staircase. And Kythera's *"not yet"* implies at least one god is timing the
Emperor's death against something on this table.

## Collision sketches

Three ways the staircase can play, for calibration rather than prescription:

1. **The Conclave inherits everything.** The Emperor dies in year 2–3; a contested succession arrives at the Conclave's doorstep in year 4; the Twelve — convened to argue the Axiom question — must instead rule on succession interference while the Pale Watch reports the Number nearing zero. The Concordance's machinery takes maximum simultaneous load. Something structural gives: a rule bends publicly, and the truth-keeping edifice cracks from the top.
2. **Zero comes early.** The Number is a countdown *Palladia* set; nothing obliges her to honor it. The drowned relay finishes its work mid-Conclave — the Maze opens (or sings) with the Twelve conveniently assembled in one city. Whether that's her attack, her petition for a thirteenth seat, or her graduation is the campaign's biggest single decision.
3. **The quiet version.** No clock detonates; each near-miss instead *reveals* — the succession exposes the edited Wound reports, the trial exposes the Understanding, the archives expose the Tidy Century — and the setting's crisis arrives not as war but as an epidemic of true information, with the Entity watching its carefully-managed humanity begin, decades early, to figure it out.

## What the Entity wants (the table's hidden player)

Every clock above is, to the Entity, a *readiness variable*: stable succession,
functional Concordance, a contained Pale, humans who can bear true information —
all inputs to one output, 200 years out. It will nudge (dreams, gifts, doors)
wherever readiness is threatened. It has also, on the evidence of
[625 letters](tales/the-kept-man.md) and one word — [*again*](tales/the-ninth-game.md)
— begun, at the margins, to *want things* in a smaller way. Whether those two
motions stay aligned is the deepest clock of all, and it has no number.
