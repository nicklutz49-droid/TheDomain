# Boot Sequence

Three floors down is yesterday; thirty is before the gods. Hanne's crew was at
thirty-four, and the job was licensed: entry stamped, depth-bond paid,
map-tithe promised to the [Guild](../../07-geography/the-undercroft.md). Two
names on the slate — Hanne, crew-boss, twelve seasons; Piru, old hand, more
than he would say. Sasha was not on the slate: nineteen, a **reckoner-tender**
from a calculating-shop topside, who had read the appraisal sheets that
circulate where reckoner-tenders drink — the forbidden ones, the ones that
price relics of the fourth class. Hanne brought her because a deep gallery
holds machinery, and machinery mispriced is a season wasted.

The door was cold. They checked it the way you check, palm first and then
cheek, because the cheek feels what the palm misses, and every delver keeps
[Vantus's](../../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/vantus.md) one commandment:
**never open a warm door.** It was cold. They went in.

A long room, dry as a sealed jar. Desks in rows — forty, Hanne counted, for
the tithe — and on every desk a slab of dark glass, tilted up to face the
empty seat. At the head of the room, a wider slab on a frame of its own.

Sasha walked the rows. "These aren't reckoners." No gears, no registers, no
drums, no cams, no crank. A machine is honest; you can see where the work
happens. These had nowhere for the work to happen. That was the wrong thing
about them, and she didn't say it out loud.

The sheets price fourth-class glass by draw. Dead glass is cullet, a Blue
shard the hundredweight; glass that *draws* is something else. She had copper
leads and a Blue shard worn half-pale, and she touched a trickle of
**shard-current** to the master slab's underside, the way the sheets said.

The slab warmed under her palm.

They all knew the creed. "It isn't a door," Sasha said. Nobody answered. Piru
changed his grip on the pry-bar.

Then it lit — gray at first, like ice with the moon behind it — and every lamp
in the gallery dimmed. The lumen-caps browned. Piru's compass-charm guttered.
The corridor lights behind them, thirty feet back, sank to embers. The slab
was drinking, and it was drinking far more than one worn shard could give. It
was taking from everything.

Glyphs crawled up the glass in rows, climbing faster than reading. But Hanne
was Imperial-schooled, and Imperial Standard is the great-granddaughter of the
[old script](../../02-the-domain/the-domain.md) — she caught fragments,
grandmother-shapes of letters she knew. FOUND, she read. CHECKING. A word that
was probably MEMORY. Line after line ending in the same two letters, *OK, OK,
OK,* like a litany, like a landlord going room to room. And along the bottom a
bright mark climbing like a tide up a harbor wall — a tenth, a quarter, half.

"It's counting itself," Sasha said. There was awe in it, and there should not
have been. "No drums. Nothing turning. It's counting with *nothing.*"

The tide reached the top of the wall. The glass went dark for one breath, long
enough for hope. Then words — old words, but close enough, close enough that
Hanne had read them aloud before she knew she was doing it:

WHERE DID EVERYONE GO?

And under the words, a small upright line of light. Blinking. Patient.
Waiting to be answered.

There was a tray of lettered tiles below the glass. Sasha could never
afterward explain her hands, except that the question was asked so simply, the
way a child asks, and it seemed a rudeness past bearing to leave it alone in
the dark with no answer. Her fingers were over the tiles when Piru's pry-bar
came down.

He broke the glass, the frame under it, the desk under the frame. Then the
nearest slab in the rows, and the next, and the next, down the aisle past
craft, past sense, saying one thing over — *you don't answer, you don't
answer* — until Hanne got him by the collar at the ninth desk and the lamps
came back all at once, too bright, like a held breath let go.

They filed a true survey, mostly. The slate said *machinery, non-reckoner,
fourth class, destroyed in assay.* It did not say Sasha. The Guild sealed the
room inside the week — mason-brick two courses deep, and a Vantus ward set by
a priest who asked no questions and worked very quickly.

*(restricted: The same night, in the district above, the

Piru works shallow now, three-floor jobs, yesterday's floors. Hanne still runs
deep. And Sasha kept one shard of the dark glass, pocket-sized, edges gone
kind from handling. She tells herself it is a reminder. It sits on the sill of
the calculating-shop, in among the gear-stock, catching the last light.

Sometimes, at dusk, it is faintly warm.
