# Sahar of the Glass Banner

Union Architect-marshal at the
[Battle of Red Sands](../../01-foundation/events/battle-of-red-sands.md) and the
driving author of the **Fluxborn Accords**.

## The banner

Her command standard was a light-sculpture — a banner of structured glass-light her
engineers could raise through any sandstorm, visible when every flag had failed. It
became the emblem of what she stood for: *clarity under conditions built to
prevent it.*

## Red Sands

When the execution orders came down on day four, Sahar refused hers — the only
senior officer on either side who did. Her cohort took its Fluxborn prisoners out
of the line and guarded them *from both armies*. Nine days later the field was
glassing over and twenty gifted humans were dead anyway; within a decade she had
argued, shamed, and drafted the Accords into existence at Haven's Rest, retiring
her commission to do it.

## Legacy

- The Accords (canon: "honored in spirit, violated in practice" — she predicted the second half herself).
- Union precision doctrine descends from her maxim: *"Force you cannot aim is force you have already wasted."*
- The Glass Banner is raised once a year over the Accord Walk at the Glass Flats.

## Open threads

- Her private journals from the drafting years are held sealed by the Union — allegedly because they name the officers who *carried out* the executions, several of whose houses still hold rank.
