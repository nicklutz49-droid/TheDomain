# Saint Verilee of the Long Road

The first pilgrim — the woman whose lifetime of walking defined the
[Temple Circuit](../../10-supplementary/pilgrimage.md) and seeded the pilgrimage
tradition itself (canon dates the first relay pilgrimages to ~2,050 ya).

## The Long Road

Verilee was neither Fluxborn nor Architect — a Foundation-era surveyor's widow who
set out to visit *every* known relay-temple and simply didn't stop. Her route
notes — distances, wells, which shrines answered prayers and which just echoed —
became the working map of the sacred world; the Temple Circuit is, in essence, her
itinerary with the boring parts cut.

## Legacy

- Patron saint of pilgrims, surveyors, and everyone who has ever set out badly prepared and kept going anyway.
- Her **walking staff** rests at [Breccia's](../../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/breccia.md) shrine in Crossroads; touching it before a Grand Pilgrimage is held to halve the road's malice. Pilgrim slang for dying en route: *"handing Verilee the staff."*
- The unpowered saint matters doctrinally: proof the sacred roads belong to ordinary feet, not just the gifted.

## Open threads

- Her original route notes list **three shrines that appear on no modern map**. Pilgrim scholars call them Verilee's Lost Three and mostly agree they're transcription errors. Mostly.
