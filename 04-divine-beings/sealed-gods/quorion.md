# Quorion — The Honest Measure

**Sealed ~150 years ago**, in the event publicly remembered as the
[Verantum Ascension](../../01-foundation/events/the-verantum-ascension.md).

Quorion was a Union-aligned minor god of scales, standards, and honest measures —
patron of market inspectors, beloved and boring. His flaw was consistency: he could
not tolerate a claim that didn't reconcile, including claims about gods.

## What he did

Quorion noticed his own miracles were *reproducible* — same input, same output,
every time, like any honest scale. So he did what a god of standards does: he
**demonstrated it publicly**, in Verantum's Audit Square, recreating a divine
blessing with an arrangement of Flux circuitry and inviting the crowd to inspect
the mechanism.

The Twelve arrived within the hour.

## The sealing

He was sealed on the spot before some two hundred thousand witnesses — and the
sealing was *staged as a voluntary ascension*, robes of light and all. Official
record: Quorion the Honest Measure achieved perfect unity with the divine. The
records were then centrally corrected with divine-grade thoroughness.

Three witness journals escaped correction — the **Threefold Testimony** — and the
circles that formed around them hardened, within a decade, into the formalized
[Cabal](../../09-power-and-conflict/secret-societies/the-cabal.md) (~140 ya, per
the canon timeline).
