# Vantus — The Door Warden

God of thresholds, locks, and kept doors — the closest thing the
[Undercroft](../../07-geography/the-undercroft.md) has to a patron. Appears as a
stooped, unhurried man with a great iron ring of keys at his belt. He did not
make any of the keys. He will not say who did.

## Personality

Careful, courteous, immovable. Answers most questions with "that's a door
matter" and most requests with "not that one." Genuinely kind to delvers,
locksmiths, and anyone who checks a door twice before opening it.

## Interpretation of Service

Humanity is served by the difference between inside and outside. Guard it.

## Worship and territory

Key-shrines at Undercroft entrances and cellar-stairs across all three powers.
Delvers touch the lintel and say his name going down. His one commandment is the
Undercroft's oldest survival rule anyway: **never open a warm door.**

## Secret (restricted)

Some keys on Vantus's ring are for doors he keeps closed **without remembering
why**. The refusals are older than his memory — vows, he calls them, made before
he was himself. When a delver crew begs him to open one of the deep sealed doors,
something older than his personality answers *no* before he can even consider it.

*(GM-only: Vantus is plausibly the fragment that inherited ARIA's
access-control functions — a god of [permissions](../../02-the-domain/god-script.md).
His "vows" are standing lockouts on pre-collapse infrastructure. The ring is not
symbolic.)*
