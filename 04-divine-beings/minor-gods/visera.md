# Visera — The Borrowed Face

Goddess of masks, roles, and the mercy of being someone else for a while. Patron
of [Identity Theater](../../08-civilization/arts.md) and quiet senior partner of
the [Mask-Weavers'](../../09-power-and-conflict/secret-societies/the-mask-weavers.md)
craft. Appears as a slender figure whose face is always a mask — a different one
each appearance, each unmistakably *someone's*: laugh-lines, an old scar, a
particular way of being tired.

## Personality

Playful on the surface, unfathomably careful underneath. Never mocks. Treats
every face — worn, carved, or born — as something entrusted.

## Interpretation of Service

Humanity is served by rehearsal. Let them practice being braver, kinder,
stranger than they are, until the practice takes.

## Worship and territory

Backstage shrines in every Confederacy theater; the Mask-Weavers leave her the
first shaving of every commission. Actors touch their mask's brow to hers before
a debut. On the [Bare Days](../../08-civilization/calendar.md) her shrines are
covered — the one god politely looked away from, five days a year.

## The collection

Visera's masks are donated faces — the dying may will her their face, and the
honor is real: to be worn by the god is to be *rehearsed* by strangers forever,
your particular courage or humor lent out to whoever needs it. The Weavers keep
her registry. It is the Confederacy's other great ledger, and defaulting on it
is impossible, because the collateral is your face.

## Secret (restricted)

In the deep registry there is one mask no one donated: a woman's face, older,
serene, catalogued in the oldest hand simply as **"not yet."** Visera does not
wear it. She is *keeping* it — and she has begun, in recent decades, to check on
it more often.

## Open threads

- The [First Face](../../05-empowered-humans/legendary-figures/the-first-face.md) legend never names who suggested the carving. Visera has never claimed it. The Weavers have never asked her not to.
