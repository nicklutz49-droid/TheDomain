# Lorn — The Last Lamp

God of the border between the lit world and [the Silence](../../07-geography/the-silence.md)
— worshipped along the frontier he can never cross. Appears as a lean, wind-worn
figure carrying a storm-lantern; the lantern's light ends in a knife-straight
line, and the line is the border.

## Personality

Steady, laconic, frontier-mannered. Talks weather and water with the border
folk like any neighbor. Never once, in anyone's memory, has he faced the lit
world while speaking. He talks with his back to it, looking in.

## Interpretation of Service

Keep the light where the light ends. Someone must hold the edge — for those who
leave, and for those who come back out.

## Worship and territory

Lamp-cairns along the deadzone's rim. Traders crossing into the Silence leave a
wick and a coin; those coming out light the wick if they made it, and frontier
families read the cairns each morning the way harbor towns read tide-boards.
The [Silence Guardians](../../09-power-and-conflict/secret-societies/the-silence-guardians.md)
tolerate him warily — a god loitering at the border of the god-free country is
either its sentry or its siege, and they've never decided which.

## Secret (restricted)

Lorn envies the Silence. Inside the border is the only place on Earth where the
Domain's ceaseless undertone — the hum every god lives inside like blood-noise —
**stops**. He has stood at the line for centuries listening to the quiet the way
the starving watch bread. He knows what crossing would mean: gods are sustained
by the field; the Silence would unmake him, slowly or quickly, no one knows.

He has begun to wonder whether that is an argument against.
