# Grandmother Cedar — The Rooted Sky

God-queen of [Roothold](../../07-geography/the-americas/roothold.md), a
confederation of forest nations in the continent's rain-green northwest. Appears as
an immense old woman of bark-seamed skin, moss-shawled, always seated — because she
is, in a real sense, planted.

## Personality

Warm, glacial, and absolute. Thinks in growing seasons, forgives almost
everything twice, and exiles on the third offense without anger or appeal.

## Interpretation of Service

Humanity is a grove. Tend it, thin it gently, and never let one tree take the
light from all the others.

## Rule

Roothold's capital is a living forest she grows to need — bridges that thicken
under traffic, halls that bloom for festivals. Law is made in **consensus
groves**, where disputes are argued until the canopy itself is said to lean.
Felling a living tree without her leave is the one unforgivable crime.

## Secret (restricted)

Her oldest grove — the one she grew first, before she remembered anything —
stands in a **perfect hexagonal lattice**, tree by tree, to survey-grade
precision. She calls it the First Planting and does not question it. Beneath it
sits a surface relay; her roots grew along its field lines. Grandmother Cedar has
spent three millennia unknowingly cultivating a temple, and her deepest roots
touch [god-script](../../02-the-domain/god-script.md)-etched casing she has never
told anyone she can feel.

## Connections

- [Tezcamotl](tezcamotl.md) — southern neighbor; she considers being *seen* constantly a kind of clear-cutting of the soul
- [The Free Coasts](../../07-geography/the-americas/the-free-coasts.md) — she permits one trade road through her forests and audits it personally

## Open threads

- She is arguably the only god physically *touching* Domain infrastructure daily. If anyone ever explains to her what she's been feeling all these centuries, she skips every intermediate revelation the Twelve fear — straight to the deep truth.
