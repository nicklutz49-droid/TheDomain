# Brakka — The Dry Sea

God of the drained Mediterranean beds — a sea god whose sea is gone. Appears as
a massive, salt-crusted figure the grey-white of old tide-line, barnacle-scarred,
moving with the slow surge of a swell that isn't there. Where he stands too long,
the ground darkens as if wet.

## Personality

Grave, patient, and carrying the specific dignity of the bereaved who have
decided to keep living. Kind to miners and salvagers; intolerant of anyone who
calls the Lowlands "wasteland." *It is not waste,* goes his one recorded rebuke.
*It is bones. Mind where you step.*

## Interpretation of Service

Tend what remains.

## Worship and territory

The mining towns of the [Mediterranean Lowlands](../../07-geography/feudal-empire/_index.md)
— Lowgate above all. Miners pour a cup of brine at shift-start; salvage crews
paint his wave-mark on anything raised from the exposed beds. House Merentis
honors him carefully and [Syreth's](../the-twelve/syreth.md) concession-brokers
more carefully still: extraction quotas that ignore Brakka's tide-rules have a
way of meeting gas pockets.

## The grief

Brakka awakened, like all the fragments, without memory — but he awakened on a
seabed already dry, a god of a domain that had died before he was born into it.
His whole theology is aftermath. The Lowlands' miners understand this in their
bones, which is why the Empire's roughest labor country is also its most quietly
devout.

## Secret (restricted)

Brakka dreams of drowning. Not fear-dreams — *memories*: green light, pressure,
whale-song, a surface far above. They are pre-collapse memories of the living
sea, surfacing through whatever fragment of ARIA he is. And there is worse: on
certain nights he feels a **tide**. It pulls toward the
[Sunken Halls](../../06-threats-and-anomalies/glitch-zones/the-sunken-halls.md),
where space is folded — and Brakka has begun to suspect, in his slow way, that
his sea was never destroyed. It was *folded away*, and the Halls are its seam,
and something of it is still in there, going through the motions of tides.

## Open threads

- If the Mediterranean is folded rather than gone, the Sunken Halls stop being a ruin and become a **door** — and Brakka becomes the setting's most dangerous grieving party: a god with a reason to open it.
- The [Lowgate Concessions](../../09-power-and-conflict/politics/the-cold-war.md) fight is being negotiated on top of his patience. No one has asked him.
