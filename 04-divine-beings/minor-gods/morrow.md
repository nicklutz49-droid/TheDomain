# Morrow — The Field After

God of the day after battle. Appears as a figure of no particular army in a
burned coat, carrying a waterskin and a ledger of names. Soldiers of all three
powers leave offerings for Morrow and deny doing so.

## Personality

Quiet, unhurried, impossible to provoke. Speaks to the dying, the burial
details, and the ground. Has never once been recorded speaking to a general.

## Interpretation of Service

The battle is not over when the killing stops. It is over when the field
forgives. Someone must stay for that part.

## What he does

Morrow walks battlefields after. He closes eyes, tallies names in the ledger,
shares the waterskin, and — the part the armies quietly depend on — **calms the
ground**. Soldiers say a field Morrow has walked doesn't *hum*. Given the
[40% rule](../../09-power-and-conflict/military.md), the powers owe him more
prevented [glitch zones](../../06-threats-and-anomalies/glitch-zones/_index.md)
than any Architect corps, and no one has ever put that in writing.

## Secret (restricted)

The calming is not a blessing; it is a **transfer**. Each field he settles, he
takes some of into himself. His coat is more burned every decade. He is slowly
filling, and he knows it, and he keeps walking, because the alternative is more
Wounds.

And once, he failed. The **Grey Harvest** — 14,000 dead in five days at the end
of the [Shatter War](../../01-foundation/events/the-shatter-war.md) — rose before
he reached it. The [Procession](../../06-threats-and-anomalies/hollow/military-scale.md)
that formed there was officially destroyed. Border garrisons still see a column
walking the Shatterbelt on moonless nights.

**Morrow walks behind it.** He has walked behind it for 274 years. His ledger
has 14,000 names in it that he has not yet been able to close.
