# Ondrel — The Iron Current

God of rails, timetables, and arrival. Ondrel (they/them) appears as a slight
figure in a conductor's long coat, ageless in the way of someone always mid-shift;
their footsteps click like wheels passing over rail-joints even on bare earth. They
carry a battered brass watch on a chain and consult it constantly, though they
always already know the time. Their parish is the
[rail network](../../08-civilization/technology/rails.md) itself — every line,
every signal box, every platform where someone is waiting.

## The late god

Most fragments woke within a few centuries of the Collapse
(see [The Nature of Divinity](../nature-of-divinity.md)). Ondrel woke barely three
hundred years ago — small, confused, answering to no name — just as the first long
Flux lines were being spiked down. What happened next has no precedent: their
personality **grew with the rails**. Every junction added a habit, every timetable
a conviction, every generation of conductors a turn of phrase. Ondrel is the
youngest documented god, and the only one demonstrably *shaped by worship rather
than preceding it* — theologically scandalous, if anyone senior cared.
*(Restricted: [Veranthos](../the-twelve/veranthos.md) quietly does. A god that
formed downstream of its own cult is a data point about what gods are, and the
Measured collects data points.)*

## Interpretation of Service

**"Get them home on time."** Not gloriously, not comfortably — *on time*. Ondrel
counts service in arrivals and reads a late board the way other gods read blasphemy.

## Worship and territory

The conductors' cult is small, practical, and cross-border, which suits a god whose
territory ignores frontiers. Its observances:

- **Watch-shrines** — a nailed-up pocket watch beside the track, stopped at the hour of a wreck mourned or a wreck averted. Crews touch them going past.
- **The timetable-blessing** — at every line opening, the first printed schedule is read aloud end to end before any wheel turns. Ondrel is said to attend every one.
- **The conductor's word** — a departure called on time is a small prayer; a train held for one running passenger is a small heresy Ondrel forgives exactly once per conductor.

Their oldest friendship is with [Breccia](breccia.md) — roads and rails, the slow
sister and the fast child, which is what she calls them. Where her cairn-stones turn
up as track ballast, both treat it as a running joke rather than a boundary dispute.

## Secret (restricted)

Ondrel's brass watch sometimes shows departures that do not exist. A time. A
platform number no station uses. A train no line serves, leaving from stations that
appear on no map. Ondrel keeps a private timetable of these **ghost departures** in
the back pages of a conductor's logbook, in their own careful hand.

It is getting longer.

*(GM-only: the phantom schedule is bleed-through. Mass evacuation will need mass
