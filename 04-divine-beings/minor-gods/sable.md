# Sable — The Kind Dark

Goddess of dreamless sleep and honest rest. Appears as a soft-edged silhouette
of a woman, features never quite resolving, voice like a room getting quieter.
Her shrines are dark, cool chambers behind hospitals and beside dream-clinics —
no lamps, no images, one cot.

## Personality

Gentle, wry, endlessly tired in a way she never imposes on anyone. The most
beloved minor god nobody brags about worshipping.

## Interpretation of Service

Rest is a right, not a reward. Guard the third of every life the others forget.

## Worship and territory

Insomniacs, night-shift workers, new parents, soldiers home from the
Shatterbelt, and above all the **Hollow-touched** — those whose nightmares are
[technically accurate](../../08-civilization/medicine-and-disease.md). Her rite
is the simplest in the world: lie down in her dark, and she sits with you, and
nothing happens. That's the miracle. *Nothing happens.*

## Secret (restricted)

Sable cannot enter dreams. She has tried — for the Hollow-touched, she has tried
everything — and every time she reaches the threshold of a sleeping mind she
finds it **already tenanted**. Something is in there ahead of her. It does not
acknowledge her. It is simply, always, *in residence*, in every dreamer, in every
land, and has been for as long as she's dared to check.

She has told no one. Her private conclusion is that dreams belong to the house's
owner and she is not family. So she does the only thing she can: she steers her
worst-off worshippers *down*, below the dream-line, into the deep dreamless dark
where the tenant doesn't go — and she sits at the threshold like a lamp, facing
in.

*(GM-only: Sable is the only being on Earth with direct, repeated, empirical
contact with the boundary of the [Entity's](../../03-hidden-truth/the-entity.md)
dream operations — and she has misfiled it as theology.)*

## Connections

- [The Dream Archive](../../10-supplementary/prophecies-and-sacred-texts.md) — the Union catalogues what the tenant leaves; Sable guards the door it comes in by. Neither institution knows they're colleagues.
- [Omneth](../the-twelve/omneth.md) — has twice asked Sable, very casually, whether she "sleeps well herself." She said yes. Both of them knew it was the wrong question, asked on purpose.
