# Anomalous Hollows

Rebuilding the category the Streamlined Edition cut. These are Hollows that break
the classification system — not because they are more dangerous, but because the
threat scale assumes Hollows *take*, and these do something else. Glitch
Specialists file them under **Class A** (anomalous) and hate it.

Doctrine note: the [Hollow Whisperers](../../09-power-and-conflict/secret-societies/the-hollow-whisperers.md)
consider Class A their proof; [Ithrazel](../../04-divine-beings/the-twelve/ithrazel.md)
files reports about them that the Twelve table.

## The Lamplighter

A tall figure that walks ahead of night travelers, lighting lamps, candles, and
watch-fires that should not light — spent wicks, empty oil, dead coals. Travelers
it accompanies arrive safe; incursion statistics along its known routes are
measurably lower. The folk rule, absolute and never explained by anyone who
enforces it: **do not thank it.** The three recorded cases of travelers thanking
the Lamplighter agree the figure stopped, turned, and *waited*, as if thanks
began a transaction — and each of those travelers, within a year, walked into the
dark of their own accord and did not come back.

## Stitchers

Small, spider-fingered, many-handed. They enter homes at night and **repair
things** — cracked bowls whole, frayed rope rewound, split beams knit. The work
is flawless. The danger is dosage: objects mended by Stitchers too many times
begin to *improve* — the bowl that will not tip, the rope that grips back — and
households that grow comfortable wake, eventually, to find the Stitchers
regarding the *occupants* with the patient attention of craftsmen assessing worn
goods. Standard doctrine: break something small every week and leave it out. A
fed Stitcher is a departing Stitcher.

## The Census

A knock after dark. A grey figure of medium everything, holding a slate. It
counts the occupants aloud — always correctly, including the hidden and the
unborn — marks the slate, says nothing else, and leaves. Homes the Census has
counted are passed over by that season's manifestations; the correlation is
strong enough that frontier villages *hope* for it. Refusing to open the door is
survivable. Lying about the count is not; the Census does not punish the liar —
it simply *recounts*, and the number it says the second time is smaller by one,
and it is never wrong twice.

## The Apology

Unique; persistent; harmless, and the worst one. The Apology manifests at the
sites of old atrocities — massacre fields, burned quarters, the margins of the
[Grey Harvest](../../01-foundation/events/the-shatter-war.md) — as a kneeling
figure of indeterminate build. It says one sentence, in a dialect always
identified (when it can be identified at all) as **extinct in that place**, and
the sentence is
always, in every recorded case, an apology. Then it remains kneeling — for hours,
years, in one documented case a century — until the site is *tended*: cleaned,
marked, mourned properly. Then it is gone. Specialists debate whether it
apologizes on behalf of the dead, the killers, or the system that recorded both.
The Whisperers refuse to debate it. They just maintain the sites.

## Why this category matters (restricted)

Class A behavior is coherent, directed, and *pro-social* — system errors don't
tend graves. Either the classification of Hollow-as-error is incomplete, or
something inside the errors is [trying to exist](persistent/_index.md) — and is
practicing, in small ways, at being good.
