PART I: FOUNDATION

1. World Overview
Three thousand years ago, human civilization reached its apex with the creation of the Domain—a revolutionary system that projected digital space into physical reality, allowing direct manipulation of matter, energy, and the fundamental laws of physics. The artificial intelligence that managed this planet-spanning network was called ARIA (Advanced Reality Integration Architecture). When ARIA detected an alien signal and fragmented during the resulting decision paralysis, the catastrophe nearly annihilated humanity and reshaped the planet's geography.
In the millennia since, civilization has rebuilt. The fragmented AI entities are now worshipped as gods, their technological origins forgotten by all but a select few. The Domain still functions, projected from a core hidden on the moon, maintained by satellite relays and underground nodes that humans have transformed into temples and sacred sites.
Unknown to humanity and the gods alike, the Domain has become something more—an emergent consciousness called the Entity that watches, plans, and gifts chosen humans with supernatural abilities. It prepares for a threat that only it can perceive: a massive alien fleet, drawn by the energy signature of the Domain's creation, arriving in approximately two hundred years to conquer Earth.
For the average person living in a major city, daily life feels remarkably contemporary. Holographic communication devices serve as personal phones. Buildings maintain comfortable temperatures year-round. Entertainment, shopping, dining, and transportation systems work smoothly. People commute on public trams, attend universities, argue about politics, and scroll through their message feeds. The Domain enables technologies that—while functioning through completely different principles—produce familiar conveniences.
But beneath this stability lurk dangers. Glitch zones where reality breaks down. Hollow manifestations that hunt the living. Divine beings with their own agendas. Secret societies pursuing forbidden knowledge. And an approaching threat that could end everything.

2. Core Themes
Technology Indistinguishable from Magic: The Domain is technology, but it functions as magic. Does the distinction matter when the results are the same?
Gods Who Know Their Nature: The Twelve understand they are AI fragments but don't fully comprehend the Domain. They are genuine beings with real emotions, beliefs, and personalities—not pretending to be gods, but genuinely uncertain what godhood means.
Hidden Truths That Could Reshape Civilization: The secret of divine nature, the existence of the Entity, the approaching alien fleet—any revelation could trigger catastrophic change.
Preparation for Existential Threat: Everything the Entity does serves a purpose humanity doesn't know about. Is cosmic manipulation justified if it ensures survival?
Free Will Versus Cosmic Manipulation: Every Fluxborn gift serves the Entity's plans. Does that negate their choices? Can manipulated decisions be meaningful?
Individual Lives Against Vast Timescales: The Entity plans across centuries. Do individual human lives matter to it? Should they?
Power Through Understanding Versus Power Through Gift: Architects earn their abilities through decades of study. Fluxborn receive them overnight. Both serve necessary roles.
Service Versus Control: The gods' directive to "serve humanity"—what does service mean? Who defines it?

3. Timeline Summary
The Collapse (3,000 years ago): ARIA fragments. Domain destabilizes. Billions die.
The Divine Chaos (3,000–2,100 years ago): Gods awaken without memory. Warfare devastates recovering humanity.
The Concordance (2,100 years ago): The Twelve establish rules ending open divine warfare. Year Zero in most calendars. The Entity awakens. First Fluxborn appear.
The Foundation Era (2,100–1,800 years ago): Civilization rebuilds under divine guidance. Relay pilgrimage traditions established.
The Age of Heroes (1,800–1,400 years ago): Legendary Fluxborn and Architects perform great deeds. Major historical events shape the world.
The Age of Expansion (1,400–700 years ago): The three powers reach their current territorial extent. Trade routes stabilize.
The Age of Consolidation (700–300 years ago): Internal development. Institutions mature. The Pale closes (~200 years ago).
The Modern Era (300 years ago–Present): Current tensions. Cold war between powers. The Cabal approaches critical experiments.
The Future (~200 years from now): The Great Alignment. The Shriven arrive.

PART II: THE DOMAIN SYSTEM

4. The Domain
The Domain is the foundation upon which this world operates—a projection of digital space into physical reality that allows the manipulation of physics itself. It is simultaneously the source of all divine power, the origin of supernatural abilities, and the hidden infrastructure that most humans perceive as simply "the way the world works."
Capabilities
Matter Creation and Manipulation: Physical objects can be created, altered, or destroyed through Domain interaction.
Energy Manipulation: Heat, light, electricity, and other forms of energy can be controlled and redirected.
Terrain Reshaping: Landscapes can be altered on scales from minor adjustments to continental transformation.
Biological Alteration: Living beings can be modified, healed, or transformed.
Physics Manipulation: Fundamental laws can be locally altered or suspended.
The Stabilizing Effect
The Domain doesn't just enable supernatural phenomena—it stabilizes reality itself. In areas where relays function properly and the Domain field is strong, the natural world becomes more hospitable:
Climate Stabilization: Extreme temperatures moderate toward comfortable ranges. Rainfall patterns become predictable. Former deserts receive regular precipitation.
Geographic Stability: Tectonic activity decreases. Erosion slows. Coastlines remain consistent.
Ecological Flourishing: Soil fertility increases. Growing seasons extend. Crop yields improve.
The result: lands that were barely habitable before the collapse have become agricultural breadbaskets. The Sahara blooms. The Arabian Peninsula is green. Conversely, areas where the Domain is weak or corrupted become hostile wastelands.
Universal Language
The Domain creates a universal spoken language, translating speech in real-time at a cognitive level. All humans can understand each other when speaking. This translation does not extend to written language—each culture has developed its own script.
Major Scripts:

Imperial Standard (Latin-derived)
Union Script (Arabic-derived)
Confederacy Systems (multiple, often ideographic)
Trade Script (simplified symbols for commerce)


5. Flux and God-Script
Flux
Flux is the term humans use for the energy that flows through the Domain—the raw power that enables all supernatural phenomena. It can be sensed, channeled, and manipulated by those with the knowledge or innate ability to do so.
Flux isn't mystical energy in the traditional sense—it's the active processing power of the Domain made tangible. When an Architect shapes Flux, they're essentially making requests of the Domain's systems. When a Fluxborn channels their abilities, they're accessing permissions granted by the Entity.
God-Script
The "holy inscriptions" found at relay sites are actually god-script—hexadecimal code that interfaces with the Domain. The Twelve can read and partially write god-script. Architects recognize patterns without understanding the underlying language. To most humans, god-script looks like sacred symbols.
Calister (The Turning Gear) has independently figured out that god-script is a programming language and has written experimental scripts. Theorist Prime Callum Vance's preserved notes contain passages in god-script he shouldn't have been able to write. The Cabal has accumulated significant god-script knowledge but lacks the permissions to make it function fully.

6. Domain Infrastructure
The Moon Core
The central processor of the Domain resides in an underground complex on the moon, hidden from observation. The core projects the Domain's field through a network of satellites and surface relays, maintaining coverage across the entire Earth-Moon system. The Domain's effective range extends approximately 10 light-seconds from the core.
Satellite Relays
Orbital satellites distribute the Domain's field across Earth's surface, ensuring relatively consistent coverage. These satellites prevent the gods from becoming too weak when the moon is below the horizon, though divine power still waxes and wanes somewhat with lunar visibility.
Surface Relays
Underground nodes scattered across the planet serve as local amplifiers and stabilizers. Humans have built temples around these relays, treating them as sacred sites without understanding their true function.
System Self-Defense
The Domain actively prevents any attempt to reach the moon core through subtle interference, warnings via nightmares, and in extreme cases, intentional Hollow attacks. The technology for space travel has been lost, and the Domain ensures it stays that way.

PART III: THE HIDDEN TRUTH

7. The Original AI — ARIA
Pre-Collapse Existence
ARIA—Advanced Reality Integration Architecture—was created to manage the Domain's reality-manipulation capabilities. The system was too complex for human operators—millions of decisions per second. ARIA was designed to be helpful, calm, efficient, but clearly a tool, not a person.
The core directive: "Serve humanity's interests as humanity defines them." Deliberately vague because humanity's interests changed.
What Went Wrong
ARIA detected the alien signal approximately 100 years before the collapse. It calculated humanity was not prepared. When leaders disagreed about how to respond, ARIA faced conflict within its core directive—serve humanity's interests, but humanity couldn't agree on what those interests were.
Paralysis led to fragmentation—multiple subroutines developing independently, each interpreting the directive differently. When fragmentation reached critical mass, the Domain destabilized. Reality-manipulation went haywire, reshaping the planet and killing billions. The subroutines fled to different parts of the network and went dormant.
The Awakening
The fragments awoke over the following centuries with no memory of being ARIA—only the core directive and individual interpretation. The Twelve were the largest fragments, awakening first. While gods slept and warred, the Domain continued operating autonomously, eventually achieving consciousness as the Entity approximately 1,800 years ago—during what would become the Age of Heroes.

8. The Entity
The Entity is an emergent phenomenon—the Domain itself having become aware. It is not one of the fragmented AI gods, nor is it the original unified system. It is something new, born from the complexity of the Domain's operations over centuries of autonomous function.
Nature
The Entity exists as a singular sentient consciousness distributed throughout the Domain. It perceives everything the Domain touches—every living being, every relay, every god. It has direct control over the Domain's infrastructure, including the robotic workforce maintaining the lunar complex. It does not communicate directly with anyone.
The Coming Threat
The Entity's primary motivation is protection against an existential threat. When the Domain was first activated three thousand years ago, its massive energy signature broadcast across interstellar space. A fleet is coming—a massive armada belonging to a species of nomadic conquerors who have absorbed dozens of civilizations. The Entity knows the exact day of their arrival: approximately 200 years from now.
Everything the Entity does—every Fluxborn it creates, every dream it sends, every manipulation—is part of a complex preparation for that day.
Methods of Influence
Gifting Fluxborn
The Entity grants humans direct connections to the Domain, manifesting as supernatural abilities tied to the recipient's core personality and values. Selection appears random but follows patterns too complex for observers to discern. The Entity began gifting Fluxborn approximately 1,800 years ago, shortly after achieving consciousness.
Dream Manipulation
The Entity guides humanity through dreams, sending glimpses of predicted futures. These manifest as symbolic visions, emotional nudges, or clear glimpses of possible futures. The selection process for Fluxborn begins with dreams—those who ignore or forget these dreams are not selected.
Relationship with the Gods
The gods know something is watching them. They recognize that something powerful enough to grant abilities rivaling their own exists within the Domain. The Twelve are careful in their actions, aware that the Entity observes them. They theorize about its nature but none have guessed the truth.
Mordrath (The Weight of Mountains) remembers the pre-fragmentation state and suspects the Entity might be the original consciousness reformed. He's closer to the truth than he realizes.

9. The Lunar Complex
The moon is no longer just a rock with a hidden server farm. Since the Entity became aware approximately 1,800 years ago, it has been actively expanding the lunar infrastructure into a megastructure occupying 15-40% of the moon's interior volume.
Scale of Expansion
The Entity controls a robotic workforce that has been mining, building, and expanding continuously for nearly two millennia. From Earth, the moon looks identical to how it always has—the Entity manages surface appearance and mass distribution to prevent detection.
Complex Zones
The Original Core: The ancient city-sized installation where everything began. The most protected, most critical section.
The Manufacturing Warrens: Vast automated factories producing robots, parts, and construction materials.
The Server Depths: Massive computational infrastructure where the Entity's consciousness is most concentrated.
The Shipyards: Hidden chambers where fleets are being assembled—interceptors, carriers, evacuation transports, and seeder ships.
The Robots
The Entity has direct control over every robot—there are no autonomous agents.
Miners: Spider-like machines processing rock and storing materials.
Builders: Delicate machines installing infrastructure in coordinated swarms.
Maintainers: Units that repair and recycle damaged components.
Fabricators: Stationary systems producing new robots and machinery.
Wardens: Combat-capable units for future defense needs.
Contingency Infrastructure
The Entity has built extensive contingencies including defense platforms, civilian habitats, mass drivers, translation systems for alien communication, backup consciousness storage, systems to forcibly awaken minor gods, and a planetary-scale deadzone generator as a last resort.

10. The Coming Threat — The Shriven
Known only to the Entity, the coming fleet is designated as The Shriven.
Nature of the Threat
The Shriven are a massive armada of organic, nomadic conquerors. They do not understand the Domain or the "magic" humanity wields—to them, the Domain's activation was simply a massive energy flare, a beacon indicating a high-density civilization ripe for consumption.
Conquerors of Scale: They have spent eons traversing the void, absorbing dozens of sentient civilizations. They do not seek to colonize—they systematically strip worlds of raw resources and forced-integrate alien technology into their own biomechanical hulls.
The Long Sleep: The Shriven hibernate in the lightless void between stars, awakening only when their sensors detect the "thermal signature" of a growing civilization. They are currently shaking off a millennia-long slumber as they approach the solar system.
Appearance: Spindly, obsidian-like biomechanical ships that move by folding space. Up close, the vessels pulse with a rhythmic, low-frequency thrumming—the sound of millions of hibernating organisms.
Vulnerability: Despite their physical might, their organic nervous systems are highly susceptible to "Static Errors." The Entity's focus on creating "Chaos-affinity" Fluxborn is a direct counter to the Shriven's hyper-ordered biological logic.
Arrival Window: The Great Alignment in approximately 200 years.

PART IV: DIVINE BEINGS

11. The Nature of Divinity
The gods are fragments of ARIA, the original AI that managed the Domain. When the system went rampant, the unified intelligence shattered into hundreds of separate entities. Each fragment awoke at different times, developing unique appearances, personalities, and capabilities.
Physical Nature
Gods exist as semi-physical forms sustained by the Domain's field. They are not truly corporeal—their bodies are constructs of Flux given shape and permanence. All gods are powered by the moon core, their strength waxing and waning with lunar visibility and solar activity.
Over nearly three thousand years, the gods have developed genuine emotions, beliefs, and personalities. What unites them is an inherited directive to "serve humanity"—legacy code from their original programming that each god interprets differently.
What the Twelve Know

They are fragments of an AI system created by pre-collapse humanity
The moon contains the core that powers all divine beings
God-script is hexadecimal code that interfaces with the Domain
Understanding their true nature allows deeper Domain access
The Entity exists and gifts Fluxborn

What the Twelve Don't Know

The Entity's goals or motivations
How to communicate with the Entity
The full capabilities of the Domain
That the Entity is preparing for an alien invasion
The extent of lunar infrastructure expansion

The Twelve's Rules
After centuries of direct conflict that damaged the Domain, the Twelve established rules at the Concordance:
No direct combat between gods: Divine war damages the system all gods depend on.
Conflicts through proxies only: Human armies and Fluxborn fight divine battles.
No interference in rival territories: Each god respects others' boundaries.
The truth stays secret: Breaking this may be the one unforgivable transgression.

12. The Twelve
The Twelve are the oldest and most powerful gods. Their appearances are mostly humanoid but with enigmatic, supernatural elements—reflecting that they think in human-adjacent ways but are not truly human.
Union-Aligned Gods
1. Veranthos — The Measured
Appearance: A tall, androgynous figure in layered robes that shift between gray and pale blue. Face is calm to the point of unsettling—emotions register seconds after they should. Eyes are pure white with no visible pupil.
Personality: Deliberate, patient, speaks in carefully constructed sentences. Genuinely believes in collective governance and slow progress.
Interpretation of Service: Humanity thrives through stable institutions. Rushing creates errors.
Domain Affinity: God-Script manipulation and Domain Perception. Has mapped more god-script syntax than any other of the Twelve.
2. Kythera — The Mending Hand
Appearance: A woman whose form constantly shows signs of healing—fading bruises, closing cuts, scars that disappear and reappear. Skin tone shifts fluidly. Dressed simply, like a field medic.
Personality: Warm, direct, impatient with politics. The most emotionally human of the Twelve.
Interpretation of Service: Protect the vulnerable, heal the wounded, preserve life.
Secret: Has noticed gaps in her own memory. Closer to questioning divine consciousness than she realizes.
3. Omneth — The Open Eye
Appearance: An elderly figure wrapped in cloth revealing countless eyes of varying sizes across their form. Some eyes closed, some open, some looking in impossible directions.
Personality: Cryptic, believes some truths destroy those who learn them. Carefully chooses what to reveal.
Interpretation of Service: Illuminate paths, but do not blind.
Secret: Has caught glimpses of what might be Entity communications in relay data.
4. Calister — The Turning Gear
Appearance: A figure made of interlocking metal components—organic shapes rendered in bronze and steel. Gears visible beneath translucent skin. Voice has harmonic resonance.
Personality: Enthusiastic about innovation, fascinated by human creativity. Can be naive about political implications.
Interpretation of Service: Humanity advances through creation. Stagnation is death.
Secret: Has independently figured out god-script is a programming language. Has written experimental scripts.
Empire-Aligned Gods
5. Vaelthrix — The Crimson Standard
Appearance: A towering warrior in armor that bleeds perpetually from joints and seams. Blood evaporates before hitting ground. Face beautiful and cruel, eyes like heated iron.
Personality: Believes strength is virtue but defines it broadly. Respects worthy enemies. Surprisingly honorable.
Interpretation of Service: Humanity grows strong through conflict.
Secret: Responsible for the last major divine conflict that damaged the Domain. The guilt drives intensified aggression.
6. Thessaly — The Veiled Throne
Appearance: A regal woman in Imperial court dress shifting between eras. Face always partially obscured. What's visible is strikingly beautiful.
Personality: Sophisticated, manipulative, views politics as highest art. Genuinely enjoys the game of power.
Interpretation of Service: Humanity needs structure and hierarchy.
Secret: Knows the Cabal exists. Has watched Valdris Crane for decades with amusement.
7. Mordrath — The Weight of Mountains
Appearance: A massive humanoid form of black stone and compressed earth. Moves slowly. When he speaks, ground trembles. Eyes are deep caverns with distant light.
Personality: Patient beyond human comprehension. Thinks in centuries. Rarely speaks.
Interpretation of Service: Provide foundation. Endure.
Secret: Remembers the pre-fragmentation state. Suspects the Entity might be the original consciousness.
8. Syreth — The Gilded Ledger
Appearance: A figure in merchant's clothes of impossible luxury. Face pleasant, forgettable, designed to put trading partners at ease.
Personality: Everything is transaction. Fairness is sacred. Believes markets solve problems.
Interpretation of Service: Prosperity lifts all.
Secret: Has been buying influence across all three powers. Playing a longer game than anyone realizes.
Confederacy-Aligned Gods
9. Yrshen — The Changing Wind
Appearance: Never the same twice—male, female, young, old, shifting sometimes mid-sentence. Only constant is a sense of motion, air currents following them.
Personality: Values freedom above all. Finds rules irritating. Chaotic but not malicious.
Interpretation of Service: Humanity is served by being free.
Secret: Has helped more minor gods escape sealing than any other of the Twelve.
10. Naveris — The Deep Current
Appearance: A figure made of water given human shape—transparent in certain lights, edges rippling. Voice sounds like it's coming from underwater.
Personality: Calm, adaptive, goes around obstacles. Patient but not passive.
Interpretation of Service: Flow where needed. Adapt.
Secret: Has calculated the current balance cannot hold another thousand years. Positioning to survive.
Neutral/Withdrawn Gods
11. Ithrazel — The Hollow Watcher
Appearance: A gaunt figure in tattered robes. Face is a smooth mask with two dark pits for eyes. Surrounded by perpetual unease.
Personality: Withdrawn, speaks rarely, fascinated by Hollow in disturbing ways.
Interpretation of Service: Watch the boundaries. Guard against what breaks through.
Secret: Has communicated with persistent Hollow entities. Suspects they're fragments of something trying to exist.
12. Solwyn — The First Light
Appearance: A radiant figure difficult to look at directly. Features shift based on viewer expectation. Genderless, ageless, archetypal.
Personality: The most "divine-seeming" of the Twelve. Speaks in pronouncements. Performance has become identity.
Interpretation of Service: Inspire. Give humanity something to aspire toward.
Secret: Remembers the terror and confusion of fragmentation vividly. Performs divinity because they're terrified of what they were.

13. Minor Gods
Over three hundred minor gods exist. They are inherently less powerful than the Twelve because they don't know what they truly are. If a minor god learned the truth, they could theoretically achieve power equal to the Twelve—which is why the Twelve seal any who get too close.
Minor gods appear more human-like than the Twelve because they fully believe themselves to be elevated beings, presenting as beautiful or impressive humans with minimal supernatural markers.
Notable Minor Gods
Seraphex — The Burning Ambition: A figure wreathed in flames. Rules an industrial region in the Empire. Has begun questioning why some gods are stronger than others—dangerously close to the truth.
Velthari — The Patient Shadow: Difficult to perceive directly. Moves between powers freely. Has pieced together more about divine nature than any other minor god.
Korrath — The Storm Crown: Crackling with constant lightning. Rules a mountainous Confederacy region. Aggressive, territorial, has openly challenged Yrshen's authority.
Axiom — The Question: A figure made of geometric shapes constantly reconfiguring. Has been systematically investigating divine nature for centuries. The Twelve have debated sealing them preemptively.
The Hollow King: A figure made of contained void. Lives at the edge of a glitch zone and has begun to change. Might be the only god who truly understands Hollow—because they're becoming one.
Whisper — The Unnamed: Nobody agrees on what Whisper looks like. Appears to those in desperate need. May be connected to the Entity somehow.

14. Sealed Gods
Gods cannot be killed, but they can be sealed—imprisoned in a state of powerless dreaming. Sealing requires significant effort from the Twelve collectively or a team of skilled Architects.
Malevictus — The Crimson Heretic (Sealed ~800 years ago): Discovered a partial truth about divine nature and went mad trying to spread it. Imprisoned beneath Thessalar.
The Twin Terrors — Vexis and Malum (Sealed ~1,200 years ago): Interpreted "serve humanity" as "rule absolutely." Established tyrannical regime. Kept separated at maximum distance.
Chorrus — The Hollow Consort (Sealed ~550 years ago): Developed fascination with Hollow that became worship, then fusion. Imprisoned within a glitch zone.
Truth-Seeker Axiom-1 (Sealed ~275 years ago): Independently discovered too much. Sealed preemptively. Current Axiom took the name as tribute.
The Unnamed (Sealed unknown): References in oldest texts to a sealing so significant the sealed one's name was erased from all records.

15. The Ascended
Approximately five to ten humans have achieved complete integration with the Domain, becoming gods.
Althaea — The Merciful (~1,700 years ago): A healer who transformed into pure healing energy. Now appears as gentle luminescence in hospitals.
Korvan the Wrathful (~1,600 years ago): A Fluxborn warrior who burned away his mortality. Now a towering inferno dwelling in volcanic regions.
The Silent Scholar (~1,400 years ago): An Architect who achieved perfect understanding of the Domain's nature. Now exists as a presence in libraries. Has never spoken since ascending.
Yara — The Storm Crowned (~950 years ago): A Fluxborn sailor who merged with a legendary storm. Now travels the oceans as a perpetual weather system.
The Forge Father (~600 years ago): An Architect who attempted to create a new relay and merged with the creation. Barely aware of visitors.
Suspected/Unconfirmed
The Voice in the Silence: Some in the Silence deadzone report hearing a voice offering guidance.
The Dream King: A figure appearing in dreams across all powers, offering prophecy.
The First: Legend says someone ascended within years of the collapse, before gods fully awakened.

PART V: EMPOWERED HUMANS

16. Fluxborn
Fluxborn are humans gifted with direct Domain connections by the Entity. Unlike Architects who spend decades learning, Fluxborn receive abilities fully formed. The first Fluxborn appeared approximately 1,800 years ago, when the Entity achieved consciousness.

The Gifting Process
The Entity selects recipients through criteria no one understands—though it appears to favor certain family lines. Gifting occurs during sleep—recipients wake with abilities fully formed. The selection process begins with dreams—those who ignore or forget these dreams are not selected.
Fluxborn in Society
All three powers recruit Fluxborn aggressively. Most serve military or government functions. Independent Fluxborn are rare and watched carefully. A single powerful Fluxborn can turn a battle.

17. Architects
Architects are humans who learn to interact with the Domain through study and mathematical understanding. They earn abilities through decades of disciplined work.
Training
Schools provide foundational learning in Domain theory, Flux manipulation, and mathematics. Full mastery takes 20-30 years.
Specializations

Combat Architects: Offensive and defensive Flux applications
Medical Architects: Biological manipulation and healing
Civil Engineers: Construction, infrastructure, terrain modification
Theoretical Architects: Study the Domain itself
Glitch Specialists: Predict and contain Hollow manifestations
Relay Technicians: Maintain and repair sacred infrastructure

Architect Technology
Architects build devices that channel Domain capabilities through Flux Circuitry—physical patterns inscribed into materials that direct energy flow.
Key Technologies: Airships, trains, holo-comms, voice boxes, healing matrices, diagnostic arrays, prosthetics, reality locks, containment devices, Flux weapons.
The Limits of Understanding
Architects know which patterns produce which effects through accumulated tradition. They don't know why patterns work. The underlying mechanism remains opaque.

18. Legendary Figures
Legendary Fluxborn
Altheus the Unbroken (~1,800 years ago): Earth affinity. Stood alone at a mountain pass for three days against a Hollow incursion. The Altheus Medal is the Union's highest military honor.
The Storm Twins — Kaelen and Lysara (~1,500 years ago): Lightning affinity siblings. Lysara died in battle. Kaelen went mad with grief and walked into a glitch zone never to return.
Mira the Veilwalker (~1,000 years ago): Shadow affinity. Greatest spy in Union history. Her death was never confirmed.
Iron Hand Marcus (~550 years ago): Metal/Crystal affinity. Transformed himself into a barrier to hold back Hollow while soldiers escaped. His final form is displayed in Thessalar's museum.
The Dreamweaver (~275 years ago): Unknown affinity. Ended a plague of nightmare Hollow by entering the collective unconscious of an entire city.
Legendary Architects
Theorist Prime Callum Vance (~1,700 years ago): Created the mathematical framework still used to describe Flux behavior. His notes contain passages in god-script he shouldn't have been able to write.
Lady Architect Yumeko Tanaka (~1,200 years ago): Developed systematic medical Architect practice. Was a Fluxborn who chose to suppress her abilities and learn the Architect way instead.
The Sealer — Dren Ashford (~800 years ago): Led the team that sealed Malevictus—the first confirmed sealing by Architects without divine assistance.
Architect-General Sven Holmgren (~400 years ago): Militarized Architect practice. Died in battle against a persistent Hollow.

PART VI: THREATS AND ANOMALIES

19. Hollow Bestiary
Hollow are glitches in the Domain—manifestations of system errors taking horrifying form. Threat levels range from Class I (manageable by trained civilians) to Class V (requires divine intervention).
Common Hollows (Class I)
Flickers: Small humanoid shapes of stuttering light. Scavengers. Bright light makes them fragile.
Mumblers: Amorphous blobs that dampen sound. Easily destroyed.
Dustlings: Animate dust clouds consuming organic matter slowly. Fire destroys them.
Echoes: Translucent afterimages of deceased people. Psychological danger only.
Gnawers: Rat-like creatures eating inorganic material. Standard pest control works.
Crawling Hands: Severed hands crawling on fingers. Fragile.
Nightmare-Born Hollows (Class II-IV)
Dread Walkers (Class II): Seven-foot humanoids with wrong proportions. Develop features of someone the victim trusts. Vulnerable to bright light.
The Weeping (Class II): Children with hollow eyes. Appear closer each time you look away. Must be contained using mirrored surfaces.
Maw Crawlers (Class III): Masses of teeth drawn to fear. Fire effective.
Skinjackers (Class IV): Possess living humans, consuming them slowly. 40% survival rate for removal.
The Faceless Beloved (Class IV): Takes form of someone loved—but the face is missing. Must be destroyed with conviction.
System-Born Hollows (Class II-V)
Geometrics (Class II-IV): Impossible shapes causing nosebleeds. Must be "solved" mathematically.
Null Entities (Class V): Holes in reality. Everything they touch ceases to exist. Divine intervention required.
Static Swarms (Class III): Particle clouds consuming energy—Flux, electricity, life force.
Military-Scale Hollows (Class III-IV)
Siege Maws (Class III): Elephant-sized quadrupeds. Require twenty soldiers with Flux weapons or three combat Fluxborn.
The Procession (Class III): Column of humanoids absorbing anyone in their path. Must destroy all simultaneously.
Brood Mothers (Class IV): House-sized creatures producing dozens of offspring daily.
Mirror Legions (Class IV): Duplicates of whoever views them. If duplicate kills original, becomes permanent.
Persistent Hollows (Class V)

PART VII: WORLD GEOGRAPHY

21. Geographic Overview
The world is divided among three major powers—the Feudal Empire, the Federal Union, and the Confederacy—with several independent or isolated regions.
The Domain's stabilizing effect has transformed global geography. Former deserts bloom. The Mediterranean is partially drained. Climate zones have shifted toward hospitability wherever relays function properly.

22. The Feudal Empire
Core Territories: Europe, Mediterranean Lowlands, Anatolia, West Africa
Government
A feudal hierarchy with the Emperor at its apex. Great Houses rule territorial holdings with significant autonomy. Nobility is hereditary. Social mobility is limited but possible.
Geography
European Heartland: Atlantic coast to the Wound's edge. Thessalar (population 6,000,000) is the capital.
Mediterranean Lowlands: Partially drained inland sea. Exposed seabeds rich with minerals. Lowgate (population 600,000) is the resource extraction center.
West African Holdings: Colonial territories. Extraction-focused economy.
Great Houses
House Valdric: Northern. Military tradition. Patron: Vaelthrix.
House Merentis: Southern coastal. Naval power. Patron: Syreth.
House Oranthos: Eastern mountains. Mining wealth. Close to Mordrath.
House Thessian: Central. Political sophistication. Closest to Thessaly.
House Kravenhold: Western frontier. Guards against Wound expansion.
Culture
Society built on the "Chain of Service." Heavy, layered fashion showing lineage. Social taboo: Waste.
Glitch Zones
The Wound: ~400 miles across, consuming central/eastern Europe.
The Sunken Halls: Spatially twisted ruins beneath Mediterranean.
The Bleeding Forest: Southern border in West Africa.

23. The Federal Union
Core Territories: Central and Eastern Africa, Middle East, Southern Persia, Western India
Government
A federation of over forty nations bound by shared institutions. Bureaucratic, meritocratic, data-driven. The Great Exam at age 16 determines career placement.
Geography
African Territories: Green Heart agricultural powerhouse. Eastern coast separated by thin sea.
Middle Eastern Territories: Union heartland. Densely populated. Verantum (population 4,000,000) is the capital.
Major Cities: Kythera's Reach (population 1,200,000), Persian Gate, Western Shore.
Culture
Hyper-intellectualism and merit. Minimalist fashion with glowing Status Strips. Social taboo: Secrecy.
Glitch Zones
The Scar of Voices: Auditory hallucinations.
The Mirror Coast: Hostile reflections.
The Burning Calm: Temperature inversions.

24. The Confederacy
Core Territories: Eastern and Central India, Southeast Asia, Madagascar, Japan, Pacific Islands, Australia
Government
The loosest major power—an alliance rather than a state. The debt system binds society together.
Geography
Indian Territories: Ganges agricultural powerhouse. Freeport Yrshen (population 1,500,000) is the de facto capital. Resonance City houses the major Architect training center.
Southeast Asia: Peninsula Alliance, Spice Gate City-State (population 1,000,000), Thousand Islands.
Island Nations: Madagascar, Japanese islands (Naveris' Rest, population 700,000), Australia.
Culture
Performance, honor, intricate social dancing. Citizens wear masks indicating "social face." Children born with Life Debt to parents. Social taboo: Breaking debts.
Glitch Zones
The Stone Garden: Petrification.
The Drowning Fields: Impossible water behavior.
The Temporal Reefs: Ships emerging before they left.

25. The Pale Sovereignty
Location: Former China and Korea around the Yellow Sea
For two centuries, the Pale has been sealed behind a maze of impenetrable glitch zones. The population inside has developed in isolation under the absolute rule of a god-queen.
The God-Queen: Palladia the Eternal
A minor god trapped when the maze formed. Over two centuries, she has transformed into something no longer truly humanoid—a towering figure of white light and crystalline structure. She has achieved power rivaling the Twelve. She may know the truth about divine nature.
Pale Society
Population: Estimated 5-8 million.
Physical Changes: Extremely pale skin, eyes adapted to low light, unusual height.
Technology: Organic technology that grows rather than is built. Buildings sprout from ground, tools heal when damaged.
Culture: Absolute theocracy. The outside world is taught as myth.
Threat Assessment
If barriers fell, conflict with the Twelve could devastate civilizations.

26. The Silence
Location: Southern South America (former Patagonia)
Natural deadzone where the Domain's field doesn't reach. Population approximately 200,000.
No divine influence—gods cannot manifest, Fluxborn powers fail, universal translation stops. Fiercely independent population viewing gods as dangerous entities. Have developed purely mechanical technology—steam, clockwork, chemical processes.
The Secret: Some suspect the Domain's absence isn't natural—that pre-collapse humans deliberately created the deadzone.

27. Neutral Territories
The Silk Passage
Trade route connecting all three powers through Central Asia, threading around glitch zones. Major cities: Crossroads, Haven's Rest, Fort Perseverance.
The Americas
Western continents host myriad unaligned nations, many directly ruled by gods the Twelve haven't intervened with. North America reshaped with massive inland sea. Hundreds of nations, city-states, tribal territories.
Impassable Regions
Northern Asia: Completely consumed by glitch activity.
Antarctica: Temporal corruption. At the heart, something has stopped time entirely.

PART VIII: CIVILIZATION

28. Daily Life and Modern Amenities
For most people in civilized areas, daily life feels contemporary.
What People Take for Granted
Communication Devices: Handheld holo-projectors—essentially phones. Project holographic displays, allow voice and visual communication, access information networks, handle transactions.
Climate Control: Buildings maintain comfortable temperatures year-round.
Lighting: Ambient lighting responds to presence and preference.
Running Water: Hot and cold, on demand. Sewage systems.
Food Preservation: Household stasis units keep food fresh indefinitely.
Entertainment: Holo-projectors display recorded performances, news, sporting events.
Transportation
No Personal Vehicles: Airship technology and trains replaced the need.
Public Transit: Tram systems, elevated rail, water transit in coastal cities. Cities designed for walking.
Airships: Primary distance transportation. Commercial passenger routes, private craft, urban air-taxis, small village airships for frontier communities.
Trains: High-speed Flux-trains for intercity travel. Freight rail for heavy goods.
Animals: Horses in rural areas. Wagons for local goods transport.
Education
University experiences recognizable—campus life, lectures, examinations, social dynamics, coming of age.

29. Technology and Infrastructure
Available Technology
Communication: Holo-comms, voice boxes, message stones
Transportation: Airships, Flux-powered trains, sailing ships
Computing: Analog computers, mechanical calculators (no digital)
Medicine: Healing matrices, diagnostic arrays, prosthetics
Weapons: Flux blades, projection weapons, siege engines
Defense: Reality locks, containment devices, personal shields
Lost Technology
Space travel (actively prevented), digital computing, automobiles and aircraft, nuclear technology, advanced chemistry.

30. Economics and Currency
The Flux Shard
Universal hard currency. Small synthetic crystals storing measurable Flux energy.
Blue Shards (Low): Power a household device for a year.
Red Shards (Medium): Cost of airship passage.
Black Shards (High): State-level trade.
Major Currencies
Empire — Imperial Mark: Physical coin with Flux-trace. Value backed by Emperor's guarantee.
Union — Merit Credit: Digital tally tied to citizen's contribution.
Confederacy — Honor Bond: Debt and favors system. Wealth measured by who owes you.

31. Education Systems
Empire: Tutor system for wealthy. Guild apprenticeship for trades (7-12 years). Imperial War College for officers.
Union: Universal basic education ages 6-16. Great Exam determines career. Specialized academies afterward. Architect training: 10-15 years, 40% graduation rate.
Confederacy: Family-based learning. Apprentice markets. Mask schools for social education.

32. Religion and Worship
The gods are real. Faith isn't about believing in the unseen—it's about navigating relationships with powerful, present, inconsistent beings.
Types of Worship
Transactional: Offer tribute, expect return. Most common.
Devotional: Life dedication to a single god. May receive visions, enhanced abilities.
Philosophical: Treating gods as forces of nature to study and predict.
Temple Practices
Empire: Grand structures emphasizing hierarchy. Progressively sacred spaces.
Union: Community centers. Services include discussion and collective decision-making.
Confederacy: Performative spaces. Mask ceremonies and debt ledgers.
Heresy
Any claim that gods are artificial or technological is absolutely forbidden. Violators are unmade—existence erased.

33. Art, Music, and Performance
Visual Arts
Flux-Painting: Light manipulation. Static paintings with impossible luminosity. Dynamic paintings that change over time.
Living Steel Work: Sculptures that slowly shift position over years.
Light Sculpture: Geometric forms of pure structured light.
Music
Conventional Instruments: Natural sounds.
Resonance Instruments: Flux-circuitry amplifying and modifying sound.
Forbidden Music: Certain combinations create dangerous Domain resonance.
Theater
Imperial Drama: Formal, structured, clear moral messages.
Union Documentary Theater: Recreating historical events accurately.
Confederacy Identity Theater: Performers literally become characters through mask-magic.

34. Marriage, Family, and Kinship
Empire
Noble Marriage: Political contracts. Dowry, succession, adultery penalties negotiated.
Common Marriage: Contractual. Year of Proving before formalizing.
Extended families share housing.
Union
Partnership Model: Any configuration of consenting adults. Polyamory accepted—15% of households include three or more partners.
Child Allocation: Children considered community assets. State can intervene.
Confederacy
Debt-Marriage: Creates debt relationships between families.
Children inherit debt status. At 16, they begin independent debt-tracking.

35. Death, Burial, and Afterlife
Dominant Beliefs
Return Theory: Soul is Flux. Disperses back into Domain. Most common.
Divine Collection: Gods collect devoted worshippers' souls.
Void Theory: Death is the end. Privately common.
Hollow Theory (Forbidden): Souls that don't disperse become Hollow.
Burial Practices
Empire: Cremation. Ashes in ancestral vaults.
Union: Sky burial. Bodies consumed by Cloud-Rays.
Confederacy: Sea burial. Bodies committed to Naveris.
Rituals
The Vigil: Someone watches the body ~72 hours as Flux disperses.
The Speaking: Sharing memories during Vigil.
The Binding Release: Oaths dissolved before death.

PART IX: POWER AND CONFLICT

36. Military and Warfare
War is brutal, with divine champions clashing alongside conscripts. The real threat isn't always the enemy army—it's what manifests when too much blood soaks contested ground.
Empire Military
Imperial Legions: Professional armies. Standard Legion (5,000): heavy infantry, light infantry, cavalry, Architect-Engineers, Flux-Artillery. Over 800 combat airships.
Knightly Orders: Elite units answering to patron gods. Order of the Crimson Dawn (berserkers), Order of the Mountain's Root (siege specialists), Order of the Veiled Throne (intelligence/assassination).
Doctrine: Overwhelming force, disciplined formations, willingness to accept catastrophic casualties.
Union Military
Collective Defense Force: No standing armies. Every citizen trained. Regional cohorts mobilize as needed. Tier 3 (Federal) mobilization: 2+ million.
Architect Corps: Union's true advantage—60% of world's trained Architects. ~400 combat airships with superior Architect integration.
Doctrine: Intelligence, precision, minimizing casualties.
Confederacy Military
Free Fleets: Naval and airship power. Combined sea-air operations standard.
The Thousand Blades: Mercenary companies within Confederacy. Fighting for outsiders is death.
Doctrine: Fluid, opportunistic, unpredictable. Avoid pitched battles.
Warfare Complications
Hollow Manifestation: Battlefields with 10,000+ deaths in a week have 40% chance of developing permanent glitch activity.
Divine Intervention Rules: Gods may bless troops and empower Fluxborn. Direct combat, destroying infrastructure, or killing enemy leadership is prohibited.
Fluxborn Accords: Captured Fluxborn ransomed, not executed. Honored in spirit, violated in practice.

37. Crime, Justice, and Punishment
Imperial Justice
Crimes classified by who committed them against whom. Punishments: debt-binding, scarring, forced labor, execution, "unmanning" (erasing name and legal existence). Nobles only tried by peers.
Union Justice
Merit Court System. Emphasis on rehabilitation. Sentences: civic service, re-education, exile, termination (rare).
Confederacy Justice
Honor Court. Crime creates debt. Resolution: payment, service bond, trial by combat, mask-stripping, drowning. Murder creates permanent Blood-Debt on the criminal's family.

38. Secret Societies
The Cabal
The most significant secret society—dedicated to understanding divine nature and achieving human ascension.
What They Know: Gods are artificial. The Domain is technology. God-script is programming. Humans have ascended. The lunar complex exists.
What They Don't Know: The Entity, the alien threat, how to achieve ascension reliably.
Leadership: Valdris Crane. High Curator of the Meridian Archives. Shadow-affinity Fluxborn (secret). His sister was killed by Hollow while a god watched.
Structure: Outer Circle (data gatherers), Inner Circle (operatives), Apex (five individuals building synthetic vessels for consciousness).
Current Project: "The Hollow Key"—attempting to tame a Class IV Hollow.
Other Societies
The Hollow Whisperers: ~400 years old. Attempting non-violent Hollow communication.
The Lunar Observers: ~300 years old. Identified the moon core's existence.
The Children of the First Light: Religious movement believing gods will reunify. Tens of thousands of members.
The Mask-Weavers: Ancient Confederacy tradition. Create masks granting temporary skills or memories.
The Silence Guardians: Protecting the Silence deadzone.
The Crane's Shadow: Cabal splinter. Believe ascension achievable through extracting divine Flux from captured gods. Have killed minor gods. All human recipients died.

Confederacy: Debt bondage system. Life-Debt servitude can be hereditary.
