# Slavery, Servitude, and Labor

## Empire

Formal slavery permitted under specific conditions: criminal sentence, debt bondage,
war captivity. **~20% achieve freedom.** Colonial territories run harsh "contracted
labor" (see the [West African Holdings](../07-geography/feudal-empire/_index.md)).

## Union

No formal slavery. **Civic Service** obligations, **Re-Education Labor**, and
**Debt-Work** create similar coercion with theoretical distinction.

## Confederacy

Debt bondage system. **Life-Debt servitude can be hereditary** (see
[family structures](../08-civilization/family-and-kinship.md) — children are born
with Life Debt).

## Open threads

- Each system launders coercion through its power's ideology — contract, merit, debt. The source is pointed about the Union's "theoretical distinction"; stories that test it write themselves.
- Escape routes: the Freedom Network (Union), [outcast communities](outcasts.md), the [Silence](../07-geography/the-silence.md) frontier.
