# The Pale Closure

Nine days of fog, and then a country was gone.

## Canon basis

~200 years ago, a maze of impenetrable glitch zones —
[the Pale Maze](../../06-threats-and-anomalies/glitch-zones/the-pale-maze.md) —
sealed former China and Korea behind it. The minor god
[Palladia](../../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/palladia.md) was trapped inside. The
population within became the [Pale Sovereignty](../../07-geography/pale-sovereignty.md).

## Expanded detail (draft)

- **The Nine Days of Fog.** The Maze did not appear; it *condensed*. For nine days a white fog thickened along the Yellow Sea rim, and instruments, prayers, and pigeons all failed inside it. When it lifted, the fog had set into the Maze — and everything behind it was out of reach.
- **The last ship out** was the grain-hauler *Kite of Morning*, which cleared the fog on day six carrying 314 refugees. Their descendants, the **Kite Families**, hold semi-legendary status in Confederacy ports and keep the only outside records of the Pale's final ordinary week.
- **Why was Palladia there?** By her own last messages (preserved by the Kite Families), she had gone to the coast chasing reports of a *drowned relay* — a temple the sea had swallowed that still glowed. Then the fog came. Whether that timing is coincidence is the whole question.
- **The response:** after rescue attempts killed three expeditions, the powers established the **Pale Watch** — a joint observation flotilla that has become one more [cold-war flashpoint](../../09-power-and-conflict/politics/the-cold-war.md).

## Consequences

Five to eight million people isolated; a minor god grown to rival
[the Twelve](../../04-divine-beings/the-twelve/_index.md); organic technology
developing on its own track; the Kite Families;
[Ashka](../../13-characters/ashka-of-the-pale.md), who came out two years ago.
