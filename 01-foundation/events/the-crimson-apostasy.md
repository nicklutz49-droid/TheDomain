# The Crimson Apostasy

The day a god told the truth in public, and what it cost everyone.

## Canon basis

~800 years ago, the minor god [Malevictus](../../04-divine-beings/sealed-gods/malevictus.md)
discovered a partial truth about divine nature and went mad trying to spread it. He
was sealed beneath Thessalar by [Dren Ashford's](../../05-empowered-humans/legendary-figures/dren-ashford.md)
team — the first sealing by Architects without divine assistance. His surviving
writings, the Malevictus Fragments, damage unprepared readers.

## Expanded detail (draft)

- **Before:** Malevictus was a beloved Imperial god of lamps and honest light — the small god you swore truth-oaths by.
- **The Apostasy:** at the Feast of Chains in Thessalar, mid-blessing, he *dimmed the city's relay-temple* to demonstrate that divine light was drawn from a source, not from divinity — then preached three days on the temple steps about "the engine under the world" while the crowd's reverence curdled into riot. The "Crimson" is from the riots.
- **The sealing:** the Twelve would not enter the city (a sealing before that crowd risked proving his sermon). Ashford's Architect team took him in the temple undercroft, out of sight — which is why humans learned they *could*.
- **After:** Imperial law codified the heresy of claiming gods artificial, punishable by [unmaking](../../08-civilization/religion.md). Whether unmaking predates the Apostasy or was invented for it is deliberately unclear in the records.

## Consequences

Heresy law across all powers; Architect-led sealing as precedent; six million
people living above a sealed god; the Fragments in circulation.

## Open threads

- Malevictus's "partial truth": he had the *source* right and the *nature* wrong — he believed the engine under the world was a captive god. Which means the Fragments teach readers to go looking for a prisoner. Some readers find [the moon](../../03-hidden-truth/lunar-complex.md) instead.
