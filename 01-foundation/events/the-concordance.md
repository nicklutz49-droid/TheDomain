# The Concordance

The summit that ended the Divine Chaos and started the calendar.

## Canon basis

After centuries of divine warfare that damaged the Domain, the Twelve established
[four rules](../../04-divine-beings/nature-of-divinity.md): no direct combat between
gods; conflict through proxies only; no interference in rival territories; the
truth stays secret. Year Zero in most calendars. Per the source timeline, the
Entity awakens and the first Fluxborn appear in this era *(dating conflict logged
in [continuity notes](../../00-meta/continuity-notes.md))*.

## Expanded detail (draft)

- **The place.** The summit met where three trade roads crossed in Central Asia — neutral by geography. The camp never fully dispersed; it became [Crossroads](../../07-geography/neutral-territories.md), and [Breccia's](../../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/breccia.md) shrine marks the spot claimed as the meeting ground.
- **Seven years of ratification.** The rules were argued for seven years before being sealed — which is why the [Centenary Conclaves](../../09-power-and-conflict/politics/divine-politics.md) fall on years ending in 7 (next: 2,100 AC, four years from now).
- **The Thirteenth Seat.** Every credible account agrees: thirteen seats were set, and one stayed empty. No god will say for whom. The Twelve deflect the question differently — [Mordrath](../../04-divine-beings/the-twelve/mordrath.md) goes silent, [Solwyn](../../04-divine-beings/the-twelve/solwyn.md) changes the subject radiantly, [Yrshen](../../04-divine-beings/the-twelve/yrshen.md) laughs wrong.
- **The Concordance Scrolls.** The written rules, with sections missing that supposedly contain predictions ([sacred texts](../../10-supplementary/prophecies-and-sacred-texts.md)).

## Consequences

The proxy-war order that still governs the world; the calendar
([2,096 AC at present](../../08-civilization/calendar.md)); the precedent that
truth-keeping is the unforgivable line.
