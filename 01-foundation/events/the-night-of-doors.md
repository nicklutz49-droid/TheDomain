# The Night of Doors

The strangest night of the modern era, officially a mass Reality Squall, actually
something else.

## What happened

On one night twelve years ago, for slightly under four hours, certain ordinary
doorways in **Thessalar, Verantum, and Freeport Yrshen** — three capitals, three
powers — opened onto the same place: a white corridor, softly lit, warm,
unfurnished, and silent.

**Forty-one people** stepped through. **Forty** came back unharmed when the doors
reverted — but their accounts disagree: each describes a *different* corridor
(different lengths, different numbers of doors, one insists there were windows
showing a pale grey plain). **One person** — Danel Ost, a Union census clerk — never
came back.

## Official finding

A synchronized Reality Squall, statistically extraordinary, meteorologically
closed. The Union sealed its report; the Empire executed a looter who used a door
to enter a countinghouse; the Confederacy declared the night retroactively
festival, because what else do you do.

## What it actually was (GM-only)

A dry run. The [Entity](../../03-hidden-truth/the-entity.md) tested a
door-to-door **evacuation topology** — the ground-side interface for the
[civilian habitats](../../03-hidden-truth/lunar-complex.md) it has been building
into the moon. The mismatched accounts are versioning: forty parallel corridor
configurations, tested simultaneously. Danel Ost was not lost. He was *kept* —
the habitability trial's first long-term resident.

## Consequences

- A small cross-power community of "door-walkers" who found each other and compare corridors; three have since had [Fluxborn dreams](../../05-empowered-humans/fluxborn.md).
- Glitch Specialists now file doorway anomalies under a dedicated code (D-41).
- The [Dream Archive](../../10-supplementary/prophecies-and-sacred-texts.md) logged a spike of white-corridor dreams *the week before*. Nobody has cross-referenced it. Yet.
