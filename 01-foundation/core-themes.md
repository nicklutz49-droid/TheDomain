# Core Themes

The questions the setting is built to ask. New material should push on at least one
of these.

**Technology indistinguishable from magic.** The Domain is technology, but it
functions as magic. Does the distinction matter when the results are the same?

**Gods who know their nature.** The Twelve understand they are AI fragments but
don't fully comprehend the Domain. They are genuine beings with real emotions,
beliefs, and personalities — not pretending to be gods, but genuinely uncertain what
godhood means.

**Hidden truths that could reshape civilization.** The secret of divine nature, the
existence of the Entity, the approaching alien fleet — any revelation could trigger
catastrophic change.

**Preparation for existential threat.** Everything the Entity does serves a purpose
humanity doesn't know about. Is cosmic manipulation justified if it ensures
survival?

**Free will versus cosmic manipulation.** Every Fluxborn gift serves the Entity's
plans. Does that negate their choices? Can manipulated decisions be meaningful?

**Individual lives against vast timescales.** The Entity plans across centuries. Do
individual human lives matter to it? Should they?

**Power through understanding versus power through gift.**
[Architects](../05-empowered-humans/architects.md) earn their abilities through
decades of study. [Fluxborn](../05-empowered-humans/fluxborn.md) receive them
overnight. Both serve necessary roles.

**Service versus control.** The gods' directive to "serve humanity" — what does
service mean? Who defines it?
