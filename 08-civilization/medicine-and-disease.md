# Medicine and Disease

Rebuilding a category the Streamlined Edition cut. Canon gives the tools —
healing matrices, diagnostic arrays, prosthetics, Medical
[Architects](../05-empowered-humans/architects.md) — this file supplies what they
work *against*.

## What medicine can do

Healing matrices knit tissue, purge infection, and regrow what the body remembers
having; diagnostic arrays read a patient like a ledger. The result: trauma,
childbirth, and classical infection are largely solved problems *where the Domain
field is strong and a matrix is in reach*. Medicine's real variables are
distance, money, and field strength — a frontier farmhand and a Thessalar noble
live in different medical centuries.

## What medicine cannot do

- **Regrow what the body has stopped remembering** — old wounds heal as old wounds. (Warden [Kravenhold's](../13-characters/roswitha-kravenhold.md) arm, lost decades ago, is prosthetic for good.)
- **Age.** Matrices maintain; they do not rewind. Divine healing can do more, and is priced accordingly — in devotion, not coin.
- **Anything in a deadzone.** The [Silence](../07-geography/the-silence.md) practices heroic mechanical-age medicine: surgery, chemistry, quarantine. Silence-trained surgeons are quietly the best pure anatomists alive.

## Diseases of the Domain age

The old plagues are gone. The new ones are stranger:

- **Static fever** — from [Static Swarm](../06-threats-and-anomalies/hollow/system-born.md) exposure: the body's own Flux drains; patients dim, sleep long, wake tired. Treatment: shard-transfusion and time. Chronic cases hear humming.
- **Glitch-sickness** — the zone-worker's disease: reality-exposure accumulating like miner's lung. Early signs: déjà vu, mirror avoidance, counting compulsions. The [Pilgrims'](../09-power-and-conflict/mercenary-companies.md) pension rolls are a catalogue of late stages.
- **Hollow-touch** — survivors of Class II+ encounters: intrusive nightmares that are technically *accurate* (the Dread Walker's face returns nightly). Treated by Union dream-clinics; the Dreamweaver's methods, half-preserved, are the founding texts.
- **Threshold syndrome** — post-[Night of Doors](../01-foundation/events/the-night-of-doors.md): door-walkers who cannot pass unfamiliar doorways without ritual. Rare, new, and — the clinics have noticed but not published — *spreading among people who never walked*.
- **The Pale adaptations** — not a disease, but the isolation phenotype ([Ashka's](../13-characters/ashka-of-the-pale.md) pallor and light-sensitivity) is studied like one, to her disgust.

## The temple question

[Kythera's](../04-divine-beings/the-twelve/kythera.md) devotional healers can cure
what matrices can't — sometimes, unpredictably, at the cost of a life's service.
Every hospital keeps two doors: the matrix ward and the shrine. Medical
Architects and Kytheran devotees maintain a rivalry so old it has liturgy.
