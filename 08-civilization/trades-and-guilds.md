# Trades and Guilds

Someone keeps the [amenities](daily-life.md) running. The Domain age has trades
no other world could need — and three rival answers to who may practice them.

## The Domain-age trades

- **Relay-wardens** — temple technicians who maintain the sacred infrastructure;
  half priest, half plumber. Since [temples are built around surface relays](religion.md),
  every rite has a maintenance schedule behind it ([infrastructure](../02-the-domain/infrastructure.md)).
- **Mooring-wrights** — [Anchor-Oak](../10-supplementary/ecology.md) arborists and
  berth-masters. A grounding oak takes sixty years to train into a berth; the craft
  is measured in generations, and [airship lines](technology/airships.md) rent what
  wrights' grandparents planted.
- **Stasis-smiths** — install and mend household preservation units. Also the
  neutral party summoned when an [heirloom meal](cuisine.md) is disputed in an
  estate settlement: whoever breaks the seal, it should be a professional.
- **Lumen-farmers** — keepers of the street-lighting orchards, pruning
  [Lumen-Trees](../10-supplementary/ecology.md) so whole avenues pulse in step
  with the local relay.
- **Glitch-pickers** — licensed edge-of-zone salvage crews
  ([zones](../06-threats-and-anomalies/glitch-zones/_index.md)). Short careers,
  excellent pay, and the oldest occupational pension scheme in the world — see
  glitch-sickness in [medicine](medicine-and-disease.md).
- **Pattern-scribes** — licensed inscribers of
  [Flux Circuitry](technology/flux-circuitry.md). They copy working patterns
  exactly; only [Architects](../05-empowered-humans/architects.md) may deviate.
- **Reckoner-tenders** — machinists of the great
  [analog computers](technology/analog-computing.md): gear-cutters, cam-grinders,
  the people who can hear a wrong answer coming in a bearing's whine.
- **Brine-pourers** — Lowgate mine-ritualists in
  [Brakka's](../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/brakka.md) service, paying the old sea
  back a libation for every ton of salt lifted out of its drained bed.
- **Echo-hunters** — trainers and handlers of
  [Echo-Birds](../10-supplementary/ecology.md). Employed by everyone, trusted by
  no one; every contract includes a clause about what the bird heard on the way in.
- **Delvers** — the licensed under-city trade; see
  [the Undercroft](../07-geography/the-undercroft.md) for the Guild, the
  depth-bonds, and the one law.

## Three ways to own a craft

- **Feudal Empire — guild-chains.** Each guild is a link in the Chain of Service:
  it owes its craft upward and owes training downward. [Apprenticeship runs 7–12
  years](education.md); mastery is marked with a stamp blessed at a
  [Cindrath](../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/cindrath.md) forge-shrine, and a
  revoked stamp is a small excommunication.
- **Federal Union — licensure boards.** The [Great Exam](education.md) places
  candidates; boards examine, license, audit, and revoke. No mysteries, no
  masters — a license is a document, renewable every fifth year
  ([Union](../07-geography/federal-union.md)).
- **Confederacy — family-craft.** Trades are inherited like debts, and traded in
  the apprentice markets, where a family sells a child's training years against a
  share of future earnings ([Confederacy](../07-geography/confederacy.md)). The
  craft-mask a journeyman earns is often the first mask they own outright.

## Guild politics worth stories

- **Berth fees.** Mooring-wrights control a resource that cannot be built, only
  inherited — and the [airship lines](technology/airships.md) are done asking.
  Three ports have tried planting company oaks. The oldest is forty years from
  holding a skiff.
- **The map-tithe.** The [Delvers' Guild's](../07-geography/the-undercroft.md)
  composite maps make it the silent partner of every city that wants to dig a
  foundation. Selling a single quarter's sheet has started wars between houses.
- **The standards war.** Pattern-scribes are split between Union board notation
  and Empire guild-marks for annotating [circuitry](technology/flux-circuitry.md).
  The same inscription, documented two ways, and neither side's copyists can
  legally maintain the other's work — border towns pay double.
- **The wardens' silence.** Relay-wardens, more than anyone alive, notice when
  the sacred machinery behaves oddly — and there is no desk in any
  [temple](religion.md) where "the god's works are malfunctioning" is not heresy.
  So they keep private logs, and trade them, warden to warden, in a quiet argot
  of "weather." *(Restricted: several warden logs note anomaly clusters on the
  nights before the [Night of Doors](../01-foundation/events/the-night-of-doors.md).
  No one has collated them. Yet.)*
