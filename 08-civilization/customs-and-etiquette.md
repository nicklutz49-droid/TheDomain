# Customs and Etiquette

The small rules that make the powers feel different to stand in.

## Introductions

- **Empire:** name, house or trade, and *chain* — who you serve and who serves you. Omitting your chain reads as either royalty or vagrancy.
- **Union:** name and contribution ("Reza, water systems"). Titles are for documents; asking someone's Exam score is obscene.
- **Confederacy:** name and standing debts ("Wren's-debt, twice over"). Introducing yourself with no debts at all means you're either divine or lying.

## Gifts

- **Empire:** gifts must be *useful* — the waste taboo makes ornament suspect. The perfect gift is something the recipient would have had to buy.
- **Union:** gifts must be *transparent* — given openly, logged if official. Anonymous gifts are treated as attempted corruption or courtship, and etiquette books genuinely advise determining which before responding.
- **Confederacy:** every gift creates debt, so gifts are *negotiations*. Declining is an art: refuse the object, honor the offer. Only Bare-Day gifts are debt-free.

## Oaths and thresholds

- Oaths sworn by a god invoke that god's *attention* — perjury under a god-oath is between you and the temple, not the court. The old lamp-oath ("by honest light") quietly fell out of use ~800 years ago; almost no one remembers whose oath it was.
- Threshold customs are Hollow-logic: mirrors over lintels, a lamp lit before dusk, and the near-universal *knock spoken aloud* — announcing yourself so that whatever answers has to take a shape first. Since the [Night of Doors](../01-foundation/events/the-night-of-doors.md), leaving a door ajar with a lamp behind it carries new meaning.

## Speaking of the divine

Casual god-invocation is regionally calibrated: Imperials swear by their patron
constantly (familiarity as fealty); Union speech avoids god-names in professional
contexts (church–state hygiene); Confederates address gods like difficult senior
relatives. Everywhere, speculating aloud about *what gods are* is the conversation
that makes rooms go quiet — heresy law's real enforcement is social.

## Fluxborn etiquette

Asking a Fluxborn's affinity is intimate — it asks *what do you most deeply value*
([affinities are keyed to core values](../05-empowered-humans/fluxborn.md)).
Between strangers it's rude; between friends, a milestone; from an official, a
demand backed by law.

## Mourning

Mourning colors: Empire ash-grey, Union undyed white, Confederacy unmasked (the
bare face *is* the mourning dress — grief outranks the social face). All three
cultures observe the Vigil and the Speaking ([death customs](death-and-afterlife.md));
travelers' rites default to the deceased's custom, not the local one — one of the
few points of universal etiquette agreement.
