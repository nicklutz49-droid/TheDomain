# The Calendar

A draft formalization of in-world timekeeping — resolving the open task flagged in
[conventions](../00-meta/conventions.md). The source gave only "Year Zero = the
Concordance."

## The era

Years are counted **AC — After Concordance**. The present year is **2,096 AC**.
(Anchors: Shatter War ends 1,822 AC; Pale Closure 1,896 AC; Verantum Ascension
1,946 AC; Night of Doors 2,084 AC; next Centenary Conclave 2,100 AC.)

## The year

**12 months of 30 days, plus the five Still Days** — 365 days. Months track the
moon closely enough that "month" and "moon" are casual synonyms; since divine power
waxes with lunar visibility, the calendar is also a **liturgy of divine strength**,
and everyone from generals to gamblers reads it that way.

## The Still Days

Five intercalary days at year's end, when the moon runs darkest and the gods are at
their weakest ebb. Universal customs, older than borders:

- **No contracts.** Nothing signed during the Still Days binds. Courts everywhere honor this.
- **Vigils.** Lamps burn all night; [Hollow](../06-threats-and-anomalies/hollow/_index.md) superstition peaks (statistically justified — thin-field nights are bad nights).
- **In the Confederacy: the Bare Days** — the only days masks come off in public. Intimate, chaotic, half-holy; things said on a Bare Day are debt-exempt.

The Still Days end at the first new-year moonrise: **Concordance Day**.

## Months

Every power numbers months identically for trade; naming differs:

- **Empire** — god-months, honoring the Twelve in "order of first witness": Solmonth, Mordran, Kytheran, Verantine, Calisten, Yrshenal, Naveran, Syrethis, Omnethi, Thessalyn, Vaelthran, Ithrazen. (The year opens in light and ends with the Watcher — the dark months belong to Ithrazel, which tells you what the Empire thinks of them.)
- **Union** — First Month through Twelfth Month. Of course.
- **Confederacy** — seasonal names from [Selvane's](../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/selvane.md) liturgy (Seed-Debt, First Rain, Full Ledger, Storm-Rest, …), varying by latitude, reconciled by clerks, argued about at weddings.

## The week

A six-day **hand**: five workdays and **Templeday**. Five hands to a month.
Templeday observance ranges from devout (Empire) to administrative (Union: queue
day) to theatrical (Confederacy).
