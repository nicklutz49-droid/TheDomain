# Airships

The world's long-distance workhorse. Aircraft are [lost technology](../technology.md);
airships are what replaced them — slower, statelier, and everywhere. Between them and
the [rails](rails.md), nobody misses automobiles (see [Daily Life](../daily-life.md)).

## How they fly

An airship hull carries a **lift-lattice**: [Flux Circuitry](flux-circuitry.md) inscribed
through the frame that negotiates the hull's weight *down*. [Architects](../../05-empowered-humans/architects.md)
call the technique **weight-debt** — the ship "borrows" against its own mass and pays
interest in Flux draw. Why the pattern works, no one knows; it is copied, never derived,
like everything in the Architect tradition.

- **Keel circuit** — the master inscription running stem to stern. Sacrosanct: cutting a keel circuit is scuttling.
- **Lift-frames** — repeating rib-patterns that distribute the debt. A cracked lift-frame means a list; three mean a landing, wanted or not.
- **Storm-shutters** — sacrificial breaker-inscriptions that burn out to protect the lattice when ambient Flux spikes. Replaced after every rough crossing; the shutter-bill is a captain's honest logbook.

## Mooring

A ship that negotiates its weight away needs something to hold it that the Domain
respects. **Anchor-Oaks** — whose roots tap the planetary magnetic field
([ecology](../../10-supplementary/ecology.md)) — ground a moored lattice naturally, so
cities plant mooring groves a generation before they need them: urban planning on tree
time ([architecture](../architecture.md)). The **mooring-wrights** who graft, rig, and
certify the groves are a licensed trade with their own guild
([trades and guilds](../trades-and-guilds.md)) — and, since the Codes, personal legal
liability for every line they sign.

## Classes

- **Village packets** — one-lattice hulls on frontier circuits; mail, medicine, one stubborn goat.
- **Air-taxis** — short-hop urban craft working between rooftop oak-posts.
- **Sky-freighters** — slab-sided bulk haulers; with the trains, they carry everything.
- **Passenger liners** — the famous names. The pilgrim liner **Verilee's Promise** works the [Crossroads](../../07-geography/crossroads.md) run, timed to the Temple Circuit ([Saint Verilee](../../05-empowered-humans/legendary-figures/saint-verilee.md)); the Union grain-liner **Green Heart Queen** is said to feed three cities per crossing.
- **Combat hulls** ([military](../../09-power-and-conflict/military.md)) — the Empire's 800+ split between **Lance-class** corvettes and **Bastion-class** carriers; the Union's ~400 **Compass-class** trade tonnage for Architect integration, an inscriber station wired into the lattice itself. Doctrine in miniature: mass against precision.

## Sky hazards

- **Flux storms and Reality Squalls** ([weather](../../10-supplementary/weather.md)) — the killers. Storm-shutters buy time to ground; grounding is the only real answer.
- **Cloud-Ray lanes** — the great floaters ([ecology](../../10-supplementary/ecology.md)) drift in slow seasonal rivers. Early collisions killed ships *and* rays; modern charts route lanes around their drift, updated each season. Striking a Cloud-Ray remains the one accident crews consider unlucky rather than negligent.
- **Yara the Storm Crowned** ([the ascended](../../04-divine-beings/ascended/yara.md)) — met on ocean crossings at sea-level altitude, and treated exactly as she is: weather with opinions. Standing guidance is to climb, dim the lattice, and offer nothing she could take as a wager.

## The Gilded Promise

Forty-three years ago (2,053 AC) the liner *Gilded Promise* tore free of a decayed
mooring grove over Lowgate during a Flux storm and came down through the mining town's
market terraces: **212 dead**, the worst civil transport disaster on record — in
[Brakka's](../../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/brakka.md) own country, which no one there
calls coincidence. The result was the **Mooring Codes**: grove inspection cycles,
storm-hold protocols, and certified hardware, adopted by all three powers and the
neutral cities alike — one of the only universal laws in existence. Code-grade shackles
and winches carry [Cindrath's](../../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/cindrath.md) quality
stamp; an unstamped mooring shackle is contraband from Thessalar to the Thousand Islands.

## Culture

Confederacy yards christen a hull by sealing a [Maru](../../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/maru.md)
coin under the keel-circuit's first inscription — every launch a promise to return, and
Maru holds grudges on behalf of wrecks. The Empire's answer is spectacle: the
**Imperial Grand Regatta** at Thessalar, where Great House racing hulls settle foreign
policy by podium order ([sports and games](../sports-and-games.md)).

## Open threads

- Weight-debt "interest" is real: lattices run slightly hungrier every year of service. Old ships get *heavy*. Where do hulls go to die, and what happens to a keel circuit that is never formally cut?
- The Silence has no airships and wants them; a purely mechanical lifting craft would be the century's engineering prize — or a hydrogen disaster waiting on a name.
- During the five Still Days ([calendar](../calendar.md)) lattices underperform and schedules thin. Smugglers call the Still Days "low season" with a straight face — who audits what flies anyway?
- The *Gilded Promise* inquiry blamed the grove, the storm, and a forged inspection seal, in that order. The forger was never found.
