# Flux Circuitry

Every working device in the world — [holo-comm](communications.md), [airship](airships.md) lift-core,
healing matrix, [rail](rails.md) governor, [reality lock](weapons-and-wards.md) — is a lump of
matter with a pattern cut into it. Inscribe the right pattern into the right material and
[Flux](../../02-the-domain/flux.md) flows through it and *does something*. That is the whole
discipline. [Architects](../../05-empowered-humans/architects.md) design the patterns; **pattern-scribes**
inscribe them; nobody alive can tell you why they work. *(Restricted framing: each inscribed
pattern is a standing request submitted to the Domain's systems. The circuit is not a machine;
it is a form, filled out in a language its makers cannot read.)*

## Substrates

The same pattern behaves differently in different matter, and no formula predicts how.

- **Silver-steel wire** — the workhorse. Cheap, stable, inflexible: the pattern does exactly one thing forever.
- **Grown crystal** — holds fine detail; the medium for anything precise, from diagnostic arrays to [analog computing](analog-computing.md) cores.
- **Living Steel** — smelted from [Iron-Vines](../../10-supplementary/ecology.md), it keeps a trace of growth in it. Patterns inscribed in Living Steel *self-adjust*, drifting back to spec after damage. Prosthetics and personal shields demand it; it costs accordingly.

Why substrate matters is the oldest open question in the trade. The honest answer taught in
every academy: "the Domain prefers some materials, and we have lists."

## The pattern libraries

The discipline is a library, not a science. Its founding shelf is the **Vance Codices** — the
canonical pattern collection descending from Theorist Prime
[Callum Vance's](../../05-empowered-humans/legendary-figures/callum-vance.md) mathematical framework,
seventeen centuries old and still the root of every working notation. Everything since is
annotation: guild **master-patterns** held like trade secrets and dowries, Union academy
addenda, Confederate house-books that never leave the family vault.

The notation itself is a war. The Federal Union teaches **standard notation** — numbered,
indexed, exam-testable. The Empire's [guilds](../trades-and-guilds.md) use **guild-marks** —
hereditary glyph systems, deliberately non-interchangeable, so an Imperial circuit can only be
serviced by the guild that made it. The standards fight is centuries old, and every attempt at
a concordat has died in committee, because the incompatibility *is* the business model.

## Failure modes

- **Pattern drift** — a circuit's behavior slowly changes though the inscription has not. A heating coil runs hotter; a voice box develops a stranger's accent. Drifted circuits are retired and melted, always, without discussion. *(Restricted: the inscription didn't change — the Domain's reading of it did. The trade's silence on this point is instinctive and total, because the alternative is admitting that something on the other side is interpreting.)*
- **Resonance faults** — two active circuits too close together, patterns interfering. Most faults just kill both devices; some produce effects neither pattern specifies. This is the root of the ban on **forbidden music** — certain rhythms and intervals are resonance patterns you can perform (see [Arts](../arts.md)).
- **Dead patterns** — inscriptions that worked for centuries and then, on one recorded day, stop working *worldwide*. Fourteen confirmed cases. The academies keep the dead patterns on file and re-test them every year on the [Still Days](../calendar.md). None has ever come back.

## Inspection and stamps

Pattern-scribing is a licensed trade everywhere — see [Trades and Guilds](../trades-and-guilds.md).
Serious work carries a quality stamp sworn before [Cindrath](../../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/cindrath.md),
god of forges; a Cindrath-stamped circuit that fails brings down consequences on the maker
that no civil court needs to add to. Unstamped circuitry is legal, cheap, and what you find
in the wreckage afterward.

## The opacity problem

Stated plainly: Flux Circuitry is a library of answers with no theory. It can tell you *which*
ten thousand patterns work and in what substrate, and cannot tell you *why* a single one of
them does. Theoretical Architects who probe the why too publicly have a way of ending up on
watchlists — whose watchlists is itself a question people learn not to ask
*(see [the Cabal](../../09-power-and-conflict/secret-societies/the-cabal.md))*.

Meanwhile children play [Circuit](../sports-and-games.md) in every plaza — a board game of
routing and interference that is, unadvertised, folk-level pattern intuition. More than one
academy recruiter watches the plaza tables.

## Open threads

- Fourteen dead patterns, each with a precise date. Do the dates correlate with anything — and who besides the academies is keeping the list?
- A drifted circuit escaped the melt: somewhere, someone is *studying* one. What has it drifted into by now?
- The Vance Codices are copies of copies. Where is the original, and does it contain pages the copies quietly dropped?
- If guild-marks and standard notation ever unified, servicing barriers between Empire and Union would collapse. Who profits from the war continuing — and would they kill to keep it?
