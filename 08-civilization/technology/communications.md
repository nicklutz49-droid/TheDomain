# Communications

How the world talks: three [technologies](../technology.md), four philosophies of who
gets to listen. All of it runs on [Flux Circuitry](flux-circuitry.md), so all of it
turns unreliable on the [Still Days](../calendar.md) — five days a year when the
whole world writes letters.

## Holo-comms and the Lattice

The handheld holo-comm is the pocket of [daily life](../daily-life.md): projected
displays, voice and visual calls, news, transactions. What it connects to is the
**Lattice** — the public information network carried on relay backhaul, the same
sacred nodes that temples are built over. There is no separate comm infrastructure;
your call rides the gods' own wiring.

The consequence everyone lives with: **coverage maps are relay maps**. Signal is
strong in temple districts, spotty in the hinterland, dead in glitch zones. Priests
call good reception a blessing and are not entirely wrong; cartographers sell
"favor charts" that are really just Lattice quality surveys. A town losing signal
year over year starts asking what it did to offend.

Lattice traffic is routed by [analog switching engines](analog-computing.md), one per relay temple, tended like altars.

## Message stones

**Message stones** are forged in pairs and entangled at the moment of inscription.
Write on one; its twin warms and shows the words, anywhere in the world, no relay
required. They are slow (one short message at a time), expensive, and cannot be
jammed, intercepted, or overheard — which is why they are beloved of lovers, spies,
and banks, in roughly that order of sentimentality and reverse order of budget.

**Pair-forging** is a licensed craft everywhere; an unregistered pair is treated in
most jurisdictions like an unregistered weapon. Banks keep stone-vaults of hundreds
of pairs. A severed pair — one twin destroyed — leaves the survivor forever blank.

## Voice boxes

Voice boxes record and replay sound with perfect fidelity — cheaper than holo,
sturdier than paper. Their highest office is Confederate law: **testimony-boxes**,
sealed voice boxes that record oaths and confessions for the Honor Courts (see
[Justice](../../09-power-and-conflict/justice.md)). Breaking a testimony-box seal
outside court voids the testimony and, under the
[debt system](../../09-power-and-conflict/politics/confederacy-debt-politics.md),
usually the breaker.

## Who runs the wires

- **[Feudal Empire](../../07-geography/feudal-empire/_index.md)** — the **Heralds'
  Guild** licenses every public channel (see [Trades and Guilds](../trades-and-guilds.md));
  broadcasting without a Herald's seal is a Chain of Service violation. The Great
  Houses run private stone-pairs the Guild never sees.
- **[Federal Union](../../07-geography/federal-union.md)** — the **Civic Lattice
  Office** operates the network open by law: every message auditable, per the secrecy
  taboo. Since the Transparency Crisis (see
  [Union Assembly](../../09-power-and-conflict/politics/union-assembly.md)), citizens
  have learned that a network anyone may audit is a network anyone may audit.
- **[Confederacy](../../07-geography/confederacy.md)** — no central authority.
  Port-boards, courier fleets, and franchised Lattice houses compete; debts travel
  faster than news, and a creditor often knows you've defaulted before you do.
- **[The Silence](../../07-geography/the-silence.md)** — none of this works there.
  Semaphore towers and clockwork telegraph lines carry traffic at the speed of
  gears; Silence operators are famously unimpressed by "instant."

## The quiet war

Every network breeds its parasites. Trained
[Echo-Birds](../../10-supplementary/ecology.md) perch outside windows and replay what
they hear; stone-pair theft — stealing one twin to read a channel forever — is the
signature crime of high espionage; and courier work that must touch no network at all
goes through [Undercroft](../../07-geography/the-undercroft.md) dead-drops, paid in
Blue Shards and silence.

*(GM-only: the Lattice has no owner because it needs none. Everything it carries
rides Domain infrastructure, and [the Entity](../../03-hidden-truth/the-entity.md)
has read every message ever sent on it — it simply never answers.)*
