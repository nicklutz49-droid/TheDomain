# Rails

[Airships](airships.md) carry whatever is light, urgent, or rich; rails carry
everything else. Grain, ore, armies, and the traveling public move on Flux-trains
— the heaviest continuous application of [Flux Circuitry](flux-circuitry.md) in
the world, and the one most people ride before breakfast (see [Daily Life](../daily-life.md)).

## How they run

A Flux-train has no engine in the old sense. Under each car runs a **keel-circuit**,
an inscribed spine that closes a circuit with the track — because the track itself
is inscribed. **The rail is the circuit**: hundreds of leagues of continuous Flux
Circuitry, cut into the railhead and renewed section by section. The train draws
Flux through the closed loop and converts it to motion, which is why a Flux-train
is silent, why it cannot leave its track, and why track-laying is Architect work.
Rail crews are half engineer and half clergy — a **gang-inscriber** lays pattern
the way their masters taught, checks it against the gauge-plates, and does not ask
why the pattern works, because nobody knows (see the
[Architects' trade](../trades-and-guilds.md)).

Two service classes share the great lines: high-speed **passenger Flux-trains**,
and **freight drags** — slow, brutal strings of ore and grain cars that get
right-of-way over nothing and stop for nothing smaller than a god.

## The great lines

- **The Chain Line** (Empire): Thessalar to [Lowgate](../../07-geography/feudal-empire/_index.md),
  the spine of Imperial commerce. Passenger consists are lineage-liveried — saloon
  cars ranked and colored by Chain of Service, so a platform-watcher can read a
  train's passengers before it stops.
- **The Meridian Line** (Union): Verantum through
  [Kythera's Reach](../../07-geography/federal-union.md) to the Persian Gate. The
  timetable is a civic religion; the working day is set by it, and *"the Meridian
  is late"* is Union shorthand for the end of the world.
- **The Green Line** (Union): the agricultural web that feeds Verantum from the
  Green Heart. [Anverra's](../../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/anverra.md) granary
  clergy ride it as auditors; her harvest festival begins when the first loaded
  drag of the year rolls out.
- **The Passage Line** (neutral): [Crossroads](../../07-geography/crossroads.md) to
  [Haven's Rest](../../07-geography/havens-rest.md) to
  [Fort Perseverance](../../07-geography/fort-perseverance.md), run under Road
  Court compact along the [Silk Passage](../../07-geography/neutral-territories.md).
  All three powers ship on it; none may garrison it. The roads stay open.

## The Shatterbelt Gap

No line crosses the old war frontier. The
[Shatter War](../../01-foundation/events/the-shatter-war.md) left the
Empire–Union border a chain of micro-glitch-zones, and rails cannot thread what
caravans barely can — so Empire–Union through-freight detours south through the
Silk Passage, adding weeks and neutral tolls to every ton. The **Gap** is a
standing cost of the cold war, quietly resented by every freight house on both
sides, and one of the strongest commercial arguments for a peace nobody in power
is proposing.

## Hazards

- **Momentum theft.** Near [glitch zones](../../06-threats-and-anomalies/glitch-phenomena.md),
  kinetic energy can simply vanish — and a Flux-train at speed carries an
  enormous amount of it, some of it in the passengers' beating hearts. A train
  that loses its momentum arrives on schedule as a mass grave. Lines therefore
  swing wide of every charted zone, route-surveyors earn danger pay, and a
  surveyed corridor is redrawn after every zone-shift report.
- **Still Days.** When the field runs thin (see the [calendar](../calendar.md)),
  keel-circuits draw shallow. Passenger services run at half speed on the
  published Still Days schedule; heavy drags are simply parked, and yard crews
  spend five days playing Circuit.

## Rail culture

Every new section of track is blessed twice. First by
[Breccia's](../../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/breccia.md) wayside clergy, who
walk the roadbed and treat a railway as a road that learned to hurry. Second —
and much more recently — by the conductors' cult of
[Ondrel, the Iron Current](../../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/ondrel.md), a small
god of the rails barely three centuries old, born somewhere between the timetable and
the prayer a driver says entering fog. Old rail families still call Ondrel
"Breccia's apprentice." The conductors' lodges no longer do.

## Open threads

- Who holds the master gauge-plates that every gang-inscriber checks against, and
  what happens to a line inscribed from a flawed plate for thirty years?
- The Passage Line's neutrality clause has never been tested by a real war
  mobilization. Both powers keep suspiciously exact studies of its capacity.
- Surveyors' rumor: somewhere south of the Gap a sealed pre-collapse tunnel runs
  *under* the Shatterbelt, dead straight, with rails no gauge-plate matches.
