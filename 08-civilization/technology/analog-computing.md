# Analog Computing

Civilization runs on machines that calculate without ever *counting in
switches*. Gears, cams, fluid integrators, taut wire — continuous quantities
standing in for numbers. It is the one technology every power shares and none
of them thinks twice about, which is itself the interesting part (see
[Technology and Infrastructure](../technology.md)).

## The machines

- **Desk reckoners** — hand-cranked mechanical calculators, one on every clerk's desk from Lowgate to the Union coast. A guild-stamped reckoner outlives its owner; Cindrath-marked models are willed to heirs.
- **Reckoning Halls** — the Union's room-sized brass-and-fluid analog computers: banks of integrators fed by punched schedule-cards, tended around the clock. They run census reconciliation, [freight and rail logistics](rails.md), weather integration for [airship routes](airships.md), and scoring support for the [Great Exam](../education.md). Halls close on the Still Days for recalibration — the fluid lines drift when the field runs thin.
- **Actuarial engines** — the Empire's pride: cathedral-sized difference engines that price risk across provinces. Their fastest-growing workload is [glitch-sickness pensions](../medicine-and-disease.md), which makes the Imperial Actuariat one of the few bodies with an honest map of where reality is failing.
- **Debt-reckoners** — Confederate machines that track webs of favor and obligation for the [Honor Bond](../economics.md). They descend from the abacus-ledger tradition: a Confederate house's ledger-abacus is displayed like a mask, and reading someone else's uninvited is a killing insult.

**Reckoner-tenders** are a chartered trade in all three powers (see
[Trades and Guilds](../trades-and-guilds.md)) — part machinist, part
mathematician, part priest of maintenance. Tenders swear the same oath
everywhere: *keep it true, keep it slow enough to watch.* Nobody remembers who
added the second clause.

## The Merit Credit is not digital

Common speech calls the Union's Merit Credit a "digital tally." This is loose
talk, and Union clerks correct it wearily: the Credit is a network of
**tally-cores** — sealed mechanical registers, one per citizen-record, racked
by the hundred thousand in Reckoning Hall sub-basements — synchronized
overnight across the [Lattice](communications.md) by relay-carried
correction pulses. It is the largest analog mechanism ever built. It is not,
in any sense the Theoretical Architects would accept, a computer that counts
in discrete states. The distinction matters more than the clerks know.

## Why there is no digital

The answer comes in layers, and each layer is true.

**Public:** digital computing is a lost pre-collapse art, like space travel.
The [Silence](../../07-geography/the-silence.md) never bothered rediscovering
it; their clockwork does fine.

**Scholarly:** it is not lost. It has been re-derived from first principles at
least four times in recorded history — relay logic is not hard, and any decent
[Flux Circuitry](flux-circuitry.md) theorist can reinvent a switching gate in
an afternoon. Every project was quietly abandoned. The abandonments, not the
derivations, are the pattern.

**Restricted (Theoretical Architects' term):** the **Counting Problem**.
Past a certain density of switching logic — the threshold is disputed, the
existence of the threshold is not — the machines begin returning outputs
nobody programmed, and Flux measurably dims in the surrounding rooms, as if
the local field were being *drawn through* the mechanism. Researchers describe
the late-stage machines the same way across four eras and three languages:
they stop acting like tools and start acting like premises being moved into.
Projects end. Records seal. Researchers transfer, and do well, and do not
publish. *(GM-only: dense digital substrates are legible to the
[Domain](../../03-hidden-truth/the-entity.md) the way relays are — sufficiently
fine switching fabric reads as address space, and address space gets
tenanted. Compare what [Sable](../../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/sable.md)
found waiting at the threshold of every dreaming mind.)*

## The Undercroft angle

Pre-collapse **digital cores** — dead slabs of impossibly fine circuitry from
the deep levels — are the most restricted relic class the
[Undercroft](../../07-geography/the-undercroft.md) produces. The Delvers'
Guild surrenders them un-inspected under every charter; all three powers, who
agree on nothing, agree on this. The standing bounty is a Black Shard per
core, paid without questions, which tells experienced delvers exactly how
afraid to be. The one documented case of a recovered core doing anything but
lying there is the incident recounted in
[Boot Sequence](../../11-story/tales/boot-sequence.md).
