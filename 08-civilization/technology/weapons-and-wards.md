# Weapons and Wards

The sharp end of [Flux Circuitry](flux-circuitry.md) and the shield raised against it.
Every weapon below is legal somewhere, restricted somewhere, and sanctified somewhere —
see [Military and Warfare](../../09-power-and-conflict/military.md) for who fields what.

## Flux blades

A Flux blade is a mundane blade wearing an **edge-circuit**: a hair-fine inscription along
the spine that keeps the edge impossibly keen, or adds an effect — searing heat, the numbing
cold duelists favor because it lets an opponent yield before they bleed out. Power comes from
a **shard-cell** in the pommel, a socketed housing that burns Blue [Flux Shards](../economics.md)
at a rate set by how hard the edge works. A quiet parade saber sips; a battle in earnest
drinks. Smiths who earn a [Cindrath](../../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/cindrath.md) stamp
sell at double price, and the stamp is honored across all three powers — the one certification
nobody's customs house argues with.

## Projection weapons

**Casters** (one hand), **lances** (two hands, longer reach), and battery-scale
**Flux-Artillery** — the Imperial Legions' pride — all do the same thing at different sizes:
shape raw Flux into a bolt and throw it. Range and cut are decided by the pattern, not the
barrel, so an artillery crew's real weapon is its pattern-book.

The constraint is ammunition. Every volley burns shards — Red Shards for artillery, whole
crates of them — so quartermasters think like bankers and campaigns are budgeted like loans.
Soldiers call a battle decided by supply rather than courage a **shard-count war**; see
[Economics](../economics.md). It is why sieges still end with ladders and knives.

## Personal shields

Standard kit, rarely worn. A personal shield projects a skin of resistance that turns blades
and bleeds bolts — and announces, by its telltale shimmer, that you expect to be attacked.
Officers wear them on campaign, nobles at court, paid duelists always. Everyone else finds
the shimmer insulting, which produced **shield courtesy**: offering to power down when meeting
someone unshielded, as one would remove a mask in the Confederacy — see
[Customs and Etiquette](../customs-and-etiquette.md).

The known gap: shields resist *shaped* Flux, and a strong [Fluxborn](../../05-empowered-humans/fluxborn.md)
affinity doesn't shape so much as *insist*. Against a serious Fluxborn, a shield buys seconds.
Doctrine says spend them running.

## Wards

Defense against what walls can't stop.

- **Household.** A **mirror lintel** — a strip of polished mirror set over door and window —
  exploits what hunts by night: [the Weeping](../../06-threats-and-anomalies/hollow/nightmare-born.md)
  cannot cross a reflecting threshold, and Dread Walkers hesitate at their own image long
  enough to matter. Threshold customs (salt lines, invited-guest greetings) blur folk habit
  and real ward — see [Customs and Etiquette](../customs-and-etiquette.md).
- **Professional.** **Reality locks** anchor local physics against tampering — no phasing
  through, no glitching past. Vault doors carry them, prison cells for Fluxborn carry them,
  and every relay-temple carries a civic-grade lock by treaty (see
  [Communications](communications.md)). Hollow containment is its own trade: **mirror-cages**
  for Nightmare-Born, and **math-locks** — recursive problems with no terminating solution,
  computed on [analog engines](analog-computing.md) — to occupy
  [Geometrics](../../06-threats-and-anomalies/hollow/system-born.md).
- **The sealing lineage.** *(restricted: the Architect god-sealing technique descends from
  reality-lock craft — a lock scaled up until it can hold a god. The line runs through
  [Dren Ashford's](../../05-empowered-humans/legendary-figures/dren-ashford.md) team, which
  is why lock-wrights are quietly watched by every power.)*

## Arms control

Three rules hold across all borders:

1. **The Fluxborn Accords** — captured Fluxborn are ransomed, not executed; signed at
   Haven's Rest after the [Battle of Red Sands](../../01-foundation/events/battle-of-red-sands.md).
   Honored in spirit, violated in practice.
2. **The one universal war crime** — any weapon that targets relays or the
   [Domain](../../02-the-domain/the-domain.md) itself. No trial, no ransom; every power
   executes for it, because everyone loses if the field fails. *(restricted: research into
   such weapons has a way of ending in accidents no one investigates.)*
3. **Divine intervention rules** — gods bless and empower but do not fight directly; see
   [Nature of Divinity](../../04-divine-beings/nature-of-divinity.md).
