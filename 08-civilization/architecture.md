# Architecture

Rebuilding a category the Streamlined Edition cut.

## Empire — weight and lineage

Layered stone, vault-and-buttress, buildings designed to be *added to* by each
generation — a Great House seat reads as geological strata of ambition. Facades
carry **lineage friezes** (the family's chain of service, carved link by link).
Standard details: **mirror-inset lintels** over doors and windows (folk
Hollow-defense, from the Weeping-containment tradition), ancestral vaults below,
and the *unlit niche* in every temple since the [Apostasy](../01-foundation/events/the-crimson-apostasy.md).

## Union — light and legibility

White geometry, courtyards, light-wells; a city block is a diagram you can read.
**Status-glass** — glazing that carries the same strips citizens wear — marks civic
buildings' function at a glance. Modular construction: districts are recomposed as
the census moves. The refectory, exam hall, and archive anchor every neighborhood;
Union architects consider a locked facade a confession.

## Confederacy — water and performance

Stilt quarters, floating markets, rope-and-plank streets three levels deep;
half the architecture is **fleet** — a family's ship is its house is its ledger.
Buildings wear **mask facades**: carved faces on gables announcing the household's
public self, changed for festivals, veiled in mourning. Storm architecture is
liturgy: kite-spires for [Korrath](../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/korrath.md),
breakwaters blessed by [Maru](../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/maru.md).

## Sacred and infrastructural

- **Relay temples** are concentric everywhere — rings of increasing sanctity around the buried node ([infrastructure](../02-the-domain/infrastructure.md)); the local style decorates the rings, the geometry never varies. (Nobody asks why the geometry never varies.)
- **Anchor-Oak integration** — cities grow their airship moorings by planting [Anchor-Oaks](../10-supplementary/ecology.md) a generation ahead: urban planning on tree time.

## Elsewhere

- **The Silence:** riveted iron, brick, steam-stacks, clockwork civic towers — the machine aesthetic the rest of the world lost.
- **The Pale (reported):** grown buildings that heal, sprout, and *rearrange* — the Kite Families describe streets that were different in the morning.
