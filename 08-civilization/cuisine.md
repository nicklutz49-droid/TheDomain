# Cuisine and Food Culture

Rebuilding a category the Streamlined Edition cut. Two technologies shape
everything: **stasis units** (food keeps indefinitely) and **relay-stabilized
agriculture** (yields are huge and regular). Hunger is policy failure, not scarcity
— which makes food *meaning* rather than survival.

## Empire — the kitchen of no waste

The waste taboo rules the table. Nose-to-tail, root-to-leaf; yesterday's loaf
becomes today's **ash bread** (twice-baked in hearth ash, dense, travels for
months); bones become the perpetual stock every household keeps going for years —
some noble kitchens claim stocks older than their houses. **Vault cheeses** age in
ancestral crypts beside the ashes of the family that started them, which Imperials
find moving and everyone else finds unforgettable. Banquet etiquette: clean plates;
leaving food insults host, house, and god.

## Union — the refectory of merit

Communal refectories anchor every district — subsidized, excellent, and where
actual politics happens. Cuisine prizes **precision**: knife-work as
self-discipline, recipes published with tolerances, spice routes from the African
territories worn smooth by use. **Exam foods** are their own genre — brain-feeding
dishes served to candidates (fish, walnuts, bitter greens, sweet tea), plus the
superstition dish: a pastry called a *first-try*, eaten in one bite.

## Confederacy — the table of debts

Every meal is a ledger entry: the **debt-feast** is the basic political act
(feeding someone indebts them; the menu calibrates how much), and refusing a dish
refuses the debt — sometimes wise, always noticed. Mask etiquette produces the
**veil-course** tradition: dishes eaten behind a raised sleeve or half-mask, so the
face never fully shows. Sea tables: what the fleet catches, the port eats;
[Maru's](../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/maru.md) share thrown back from every
catch.

## Elsewhere

- **The Silence:** the mechanical-age pantry — canned, smoked, pickled, fermented; coffee-analogue from roasted roots; proudly no Flux anywhere in the process.
- **The Pale (reported):** food that *grows to order* — bread-gourds, self-filleting fish. The Kite Families' recipes are the only outside sample.
- **Frontier delicacies:** relay-adjacent produce grows strange and prized — lumen-orchard fruit that glows faintly (harmless, festive), Glass Flats salt (faintly effervescent). Glitch-zone forage is illegal everywhere and eaten anyway.
