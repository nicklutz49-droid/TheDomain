# Continuity Notes

Contradictions, ambiguities, and open questions found while assessing the source
bible (Streamlined Edition). Resolve these deliberately — each is also a
worldbuilding opportunity.

## Contradictions in the source

1. **When did the Entity awaken?**
   - Part I timeline & the Expanded Timeline (§3, §49): the Entity awakens at the **Concordance, ~2,100 years ago**, and "First Fluxborn appear" then.
   - Part III (§7) and Part V (§16): the Domain achieved consciousness as the Entity **~1,800 years ago**, "during what would become the Age of Heroes," and the first Fluxborn appeared then.
   - *Suggested resolution:* pick one date, or canonize a two-stage awakening (stirring at the Concordance, full consciousness ~1,800 ya). Update whichever files lose.

2. **When did Altheus the Unbroken live?**
   - §18 (Legendary Figures): "~1,800 years ago."
   - §49 (Expanded Timeline): "~1,900 years ago," placed in the Foundation Era.
   - Both predate/overlap the Age of Heroes boundary (1,800 ya). Note: if Fluxborn only appeared ~1,800 ya (see #1), an earlier Altheus is impossible — these two issues resolve together.

3. **"The Silence" names two things:** the natural deadzone in southern South America (§26) *and* an Imperial assassination guild (§39). Possibly intentional flavor; if not, rename the guild. Disambiguated in this vault as [the-silence.md](../07-geography/the-silence.md) (region) vs. the guild entry in [criminal-underworld.md](../09-power-and-conflict/criminal-underworld.md).

4. **Axiom naming overlap:** the sealed god "Truth-Seeker Axiom-1" (§14) and the active minor god "Axiom — The Question" (§13). The source explains it (the current Axiom took the name as tribute) — kept as canon, noted here so it isn't "fixed" by accident.

## Ambiguities worth pinning down

- **Calendar:** Year Zero = Concordance is stated, but no month/era naming system exists. All source dates are "~N years ago."
- **The Twelve's alignment count:** 4 Union, 4 Empire, 2 Confederacy, 2 neutral/withdrawn — the Confederacy is structurally under-goded. Intentional flavor (loosest power) or gap?
- **Ascended count:** "approximately five to ten" — five are named, three suspected. The wiggle room is presumably deliberate.
- **Whisper and the Entity:** "may be connected to the Entity somehow" — the source never decides. High-leverage plot decision.
- **The Silence deadzone origin:** "some suspect… deliberately created." Unresolved on purpose; see §26.
- **House Kravenhold's "western frontier"** (§22): the Wound sits on the Empire's *east*, yet the Wound-guarding house is called western. Most plausible reading: Kravenhold holds the Wound's western edge. Pin down when mapping.
- **The Garden vs. the Bleeding Forest:** a 50-sq-mile single-organism persistent Hollow (§19, location unstated) and a "biological" glitch zone in West Africa (§20). Same phenomenon, related, or distinct? Needs a ruling.

un-rebuilt cut (partial coverage via the politics files and the Forty Laws).

## Draft canon decisions (expansion wave 1 — revise freely, they're `status: draft`)

These are the load-bearing *new* rulings introduced by the † files. Each is
consistent with source canon but not required by it:

1. **Calendar** ([calendar.md](../08-civilization/calendar.md)): years counted AC; present = 2,096 AC; 12×30-day months + 5 Still Days; six-day week. *Resolves the open calendar ambiguity above.*
2. **The Fluxborn Accords originate at Red Sands** (~750 ya), driven by Sahar of the Glass Banner ([event](../01-foundation/events/battle-of-red-sands.md)).
3. **The Shatter War** was Empire vs. Union over the Lowgate concessions; its battlefields became the Shatterbelt ([event](../01-foundation/events/the-shatter-war.md)).
4. **The Cabal formalized around the Threefold Testimony** after the staged sealing of the new draft god Quorion (~150 ya) ([event](../01-foundation/events/the-verantum-ascension.md)).
5. **The Centenary Conclave**: the Twelve meet every 100 years; the 22nd convenes in 2,100 AC — four years from present ([divine politics](../09-power-and-conflict/politics/divine-politics.md)).
6. **Confederacy masks are apotropaic in origin** — the First Face legend ties them to the Faceless Beloved ([the-first-face.md](../05-empowered-humans/legendary-figures/the-first-face.md)).
7. **The Americas run on the rumored Far Accord** (mutual divine ignorance) and trade through the godless Free Coasts ([the-americas](../07-geography/the-americas/_index.md)).
8. **Heresy-by-unmaking was codified after the Crimson Apostasy** (~800 ya) ([event](../01-foundation/events/the-crimson-apostasy.md)).
9. **The Backward Saint is named Sister Amaranth** and kept by the Amaranthine Watch near Haven's Rest ([character](../13-characters/sister-amaranth.md)).
10. **Crane's Shadow / gods-can-be-killed tension** *(pre-existing, note 1 above)* is deliberately untouched by the new files.

16. **The Undercroft** is the continuous buried pre-collapse strata beneath major cities; universal rule: *never open a warm door*. GM-tier: ARIA's ground-side command center is down there somewhere ([the-undercroft.md](../07-geography/the-undercroft.md)).

## Draft canon decisions (expansion wave 5 — technology and the Passage)

29. **Airships fly on weight-debt** — a lift-lattice borrows against the hull's mass, paying interest in Flux draw; old lattices run hungrier ("old ships get heavy"). The **Gilded Promise** disaster over Lowgate (2,053 AC, 212 dead) produced the universal **Mooring Codes** ([airships.md](../08-civilization/technology/airships.md)).
30. **Flux Circuitry failure modes** are coined: *pattern drift* (the Domain reinterprets; drifted circuits are silently melted), *resonance faults* (the root of forbidden music), and *dead patterns* (fourteen inscriptions that stopped working worldwide on a single day each — re-tested annually, never resumed) ([flux-circuitry.md](../08-civilization/technology/flux-circuitry.md)).
31. **The Lattice** is the public information network on relay backhaul — coverage maps double as maps of divine favor, and *(GM-only)* the Entity has read every message ever sent on it. Message stones are pair-forged, unjammable, and "widowed" if a twin is destroyed ([communications.md](../08-civilization/technology/communications.md)).
32. **Why there is no digital, answered in layers**: publicly a lost art; scholars know it was re-derived and abandoned at least four times; Theoretical Architects call it **the Counting Problem** — dense switching logic returns unprogrammed outputs while Flux dims nearby; *(GM-only)* digital substrates get *tenanted* like relays. The Merit Credit is mechanical **tally-cores**, not digital ([analog-computing.md](../08-civilization/technology/analog-computing.md)); made visceral in the tale [Boot Sequence](../11-story/tales/boot-sequence.md).
33. **Rails**: the rail *is* the circuit; four great lines (Chain, Meridian, Green, Passage); no line crosses the **Shatterbelt Gap** — a standing cold-war cost ([rails.md](../08-civilization/technology/rails.md)).
34. **Ondrel — The Iron Current** is the youngest documented god (~300 years), shaped by worship rather than preceding it; their watch shows **ghost departures** — *(GM-only)* bleed-through from lunar evacuation-transit drafting ([ondrel.md](../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/ondrel.md)).
35. **The Silk Passage completes**: Haven's Rest (~120k; the Neutral Rooms, hospitality-withdrawal law, the Treaty Archive with its sealed annex) and Fort Perseverance (~40k; the flagless Toll Companies, Compass-Hound field-failure watch, Wastes-runners) ([havens-rest.md](../07-geography/havens-rest.md), [fort-perseverance.md](../07-geography/fort-perseverance.md)).
36. **The kept feast** is a recognized, contested Empire grief custom — a meal held in stasis at the unruled edge of the waste taboo ([tale](../11-story/tales/the-wedding-feast.md)).
37. **Reality locks → god-sealing lineage** *(restricted)*: Architect sealing technique descends from reality-lock craft via Dren Ashford's team; the one universal war crime is a weapon targeting relays or the Domain itself — phrasing that implies one was once built ([weapons-and-wards.md](../08-civilization/technology/weapons-and-wards.md)).
