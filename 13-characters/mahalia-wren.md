# Mahalia Wren — the Debt-Mother

Seventy-four; harbor-born in Freeport Yrshen; the most owed person alive, and
therefore the closest thing the [Confederacy](../07-geography/confederacy.md) has
to a sovereign. See [Confederacy debt politics](../09-power-and-conflict/politics/confederacy-debt-politics.md).

## How she got the web

Sixty years of being *useful at the exact moment* — dowries covered the week before
scandals broke, cargoes insured the season before storms, names spoken into the
right ears a day early. The pattern looks like luck compounded. It is actually
memory: Wren forgets nothing, and the Great Ledger in her head runs three
generations deep on ten thousand families.

## How she rules

She doesn't. She *receives* — petitioners, admirals, mask-court rivals — under the
lemon tree in her courtyard, pours tea, asks two questions, and never calls a debt
due. Her refusals are famous for their kindness and their finality. The one
sentence that ends arguments across the Confederacy: *"Has this gone to the
Debt-Mother?"*

## The page (restricted)

Her private ledger of debts **she owes** is a single page. Shipwrights, a midwife
dead forty years, her first crew. The last entry, older than her fame, reads only
**"Y."** — no amount, no terms. On the Bare Days she sits with the page and a
second cup of tea poured across the table, and whatever she owes Y., she is
plainly still waiting for the chance to pay it.
