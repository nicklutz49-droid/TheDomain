# Archduchess Elara

Forty-four; second child of [Emperor Aurelian IX](emperor-aurelian-ix.md); the
reformer candidate in the [succession](../09-power-and-conflict/politics/imperial-succession.md),
backed by House Thessian — and the heir [Thessaly](../04-divine-beings/the-twelve/thessaly.md)
finds *interesting*, which frightens Elara's own staff more than any army could.

## The administrator

Elara ran the Lowlands mining prefecture for nine years and left it richer,
safer, and furious with her — the trifecta of actual reform. Her platform is
arithmetic, delivered with charm: the Empire is losing the cold war economically;
modernize the guilds, settle the Lowgate Concessions, and prosecute *waste* —
including, she says in private, "the waste of pretending our labor system honors
the Chain of Service." She has read
[Hadrik](hadrik-the-chainwright.md) closely enough to weaponize him: the Chain
binds in both directions, and half the colonial ledger fails that test.

## The tutor (restricted)

Her lifelong tutor, **Master Corvin Hale**, is a gentle antiquarian who taught
her one habit above all: *ask about mechanisms*. What Elara does not know: Hale
was Outer Circle [Cabal](../09-power-and-conflict/secret-societies/the-cabal.md)
for eleven years, and walked away — quietly, disillusioned, before the
[Crane's Shadow](../09-power-and-conflict/secret-societies/the-cranes-shadow.md)
split confirmed his fears about where the road went.

[Valdris Crane](valdris-crane.md) knows exactly whose questions shaped the
possible next Empress. He has never used the connection. He thinks of it, in his
worst hours, as a door he is saving.

## The notebook

Elara keeps a private commonplace book titled, in her own dry hand, **"Questions
Not To Ask Aloud."** Its first entry, age fourteen, after a temple service:
*"Why is the blessing the same every time?"* Hale's only comment, when she
showed him: *"Number them. You'll want the numbering later."*

There are now four hundred and nine.
