# Warden-General Roswitha Kravenhold

Fifty-five, Warden of the Western Watch — commander of the fortress line that
stands between the Empire and [the Wound](../06-threats-and-anomalies/glitch-zones/the-wound.md).
Third daughter of [House Kravenhold](../07-geography/feudal-empire/great-houses.md);
the frontier is the family trade and she is its best practitioner in living memory.

## The Warden

Lost her left arm at thirty-one to a
[Dread Walker](../06-threats-and-anomalies/hollow/nightmare-born.md) wearing her
sergeant's face; wears an Architect-made prosthetic she maintains herself and lets
new recruits examine on their first night ("so you know the Wound is real before it
knows you"). Runs the Watch on two principles: *measure everything* and *never owe
the zone a debt*.

## The Grandfather

Fifteen years ago, during a bad incursion season, she walked to the Wound's edge
and spent a night in conversation with
[the Grandfather](../06-threats-and-anomalies/hollow/persistent/the-grandfather.md).
It offered three bargains. She refused all three and walked back at dawn. She has
never told anyone the third offer, and her standing order — *no one speaks to the
old man, ever* — is the most obeyed rule on the frontier.

## The problem (restricted)

Her expansion surveys are exact, and the versions reaching the capital **aren't
hers**. Someone between the Watch and the privy council is editing the Wound's
growth rate upward — feeding Crown Prince Darion's frontier-panic platform
([succession](../09-power-and-conflict/politics/imperial-succession.md)). She has
begun sending duplicate reports by private courier, which is either prudence or
the first move of a very dangerous game.
