# Corin Ost

Union records clerk, Verantum Fourth District; sister of **Danel Ost**, the one
person who never came back from the
[Night of Doors](../01-foundation/events/the-night-of-doors.md). Files his
missing-person renewal every year, twelve years running, and has turned the
family profession — the Osts have been clerks for five generations — into the
quietest investigation in the Union.

## The archive of doors

Corin has located and interviewed thirty-one of the surviving forty walkers. Her
private file holds their testimonies cross-indexed by corridor detail — lengths,
door-counts, the windows only walker 24 saw — and her central finding, neatly
underlined: *the corridors disagree in the manner of drafts, not of lies.*
Versions, she wrote, before anyone official would have dared to. The walkers'
informal circle — they call themselves the **Forty Company** — treats her as
their registrar, though she never walked.

## What it's costing (restricted)

- She has developed [threshold syndrome](../08-civilization/medicine-and-disease.md) — the door-walkers' compulsion — *without ever entering the corridor*. She cannot pass an unfamiliar doorway without her small ritual. The clinics have noticed non-walker cases spreading; hers was among the first.
- Three of her interviewees have since had [Fluxborn dreams](../05-empowered-humans/fluxborn.md). She has noticed the overlap between their corridor descriptions and the gifting-dream door. She has not published this. She is not sure publishing is what you do with a thing like that.
- Every D-41 Night she leaves the door ajar with a lamp behind it, like the others. Unlike the others, she leaves a note on the hall table, addressed to Danel, updated yearly. The notes say, in essence: *I'm keeping your file open. Come home or send word.*

## Now

The twelve-year review is this winter — the Bureau's recommendation is to close
file D-41 permanently. Corin has petitioned to be heard. Her deposition brief
ends with the examiner's own unanswered marginal note, which she obtained through
[Law 5](../09-power-and-conflict/politics/the-forty-laws.md) and quotes without
comment: *"Why was the corridor glad?"*
