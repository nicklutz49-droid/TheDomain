# Ren Okafor

Seventeen; a dockhand's kid from [Kythera's Reach](../07-geography/federal-union.md);
currently ranked fourth on the all-Union
[Circuit](../08-civilization/sports-and-games.md) ladder under the handle
**Wakeless**. Has never taken a formal lesson. Learns openings by feel and
describes strategy in dock-slang that Circuit commentators have started
borrowing.

## The anomaly

Ren's endgames are famous for being *ugly-beautiful* — circuit patterns no
school teaches, tiles laid in sequences that look wasteful until they suddenly
aren't. What only two people alive have noticed: transcribed formally, Ren's
completed endgame circuits **reproduce
[god-script](../02-the-domain/god-script.md) fragments**. Not resemble.
Reproduce. Ren doesn't know what god-script looks like. Ren just says the good
lines feel *"like the board's already humming and you're agreeing with it."*

## Who's watching (restricted)

- **The Resonance City academies** — an [Architect](../05-empowered-humans/architects.md) talent scout has flagged Wakeless for recruitment out of the ladder, standard practice for pattern-instinct like this.
- **The [Cabal](../09-power-and-conflict/secret-societies/the-cabal.md)** — an Outer Circle collector of Circuit notation grew very quiet three months ago and started buying Wakeless game records under four different names.
- **The other account.** Since midwinter, an unranked ladder account called **Held Note** has challenged Wakeless nine times, always at night, always declining rematches. Held Note plays mirror-openings — Ren's own patterns, reflected, *completed differently* — and Ren has started studying the games obsessively, because they don't feel like an opponent. They feel like annotation. *"Like somebody correcting my grammar,"* Ren told a teammate, *"in a language I didn't know I was speaking."*

No registration data exists for Held Note. The ladder administrators have
stopped looking, on advice they can't quite remember receiving.

*The ninth game changed things: Held Note played first, lost on purpose, and —
after nine silent weeks — sent its first message. One word. Tale:
[The Ninth Game](../11-story/tales/the-ninth-game.md).*

## Open threads

- Three recruiters are converging on a teenager: the academies want the instinct, the Cabal wants the notation, and something in the Domain appears to want *the conversation to continue*. Who gets there first is a whole campaign.
- Ren's mother sat the Great Exam twice ([Law 12](../09-power-and-conflict/politics/the-forty-laws.md)) and washed out of Architect training in year nine. Whatever runs in the family, the Entity's rumored [taste for family lines](../05-empowered-humans/fluxborn.md) has presumably noticed it too.
