# Admiral Kesari Ondo

Fifty-one; senior admiral of the [Free Fleets](../09-power-and-conflict/military.md);
architect of the arrangement politely never named and universally called the
**Fog Understanding**.

## The admiral

Rose through typhoon-season convoy duty — the unglamorous work of keeping grain
moving while [Korrath's](../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/korrath.md) weather and the
[Gray Fleet's](../09-power-and-conflict/criminal-underworld.md) hulls both hunted.
Doctrine-writer, mask-court survivor, personally beloved by crews for the oldest
reason: her convoys arrive.

## The Understanding (restricted)

Eleven years ago, after a famine winter, Ondo met a Gray Fleet quartermaster in a
[Maru](../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/maru.md) shrine-house and traded one
unwritten sentence each: *pirates spare grain ships; the Fleets don't hunt in
fog.* Hunger stopped being a weapon in Confederacy waters that year. It is also,
formally, treason — honor law admits no arithmetic where piracy is concerned. Half
the Honor Courts suspect; the [Debt-Mother](mahalia-wren.md) certainly knows; the
grain arrives.

## Now

The mask census fight and rising cold-war tension are pushing naval budgets — and
scrutiny — up together. A young Honor Court prosecutor has begun subpoenaing
shrine-house witnesses. Maru's priests are discovering urgent vows of silence.
