# First Arbiter Ilhan Reza

Sixty-three; chair of the [Concord Assembly](../09-power-and-conflict/politics/union-assembly.md)
for thirteen years; in the final year of his last term. Career: water-systems
engineer, then Examiner, then delegate — a resume the Union finds reassuring and
he privately finds funny, since every step was an accident.

## The Arbiter

Reza's arbitership was built on procedural patience: he ends deadlocks by making
both blocs restate each other's position until someone laughs. Sponsored the
modern Lantern Audit reforms (officials burn their failures *first*); kept the
Assembly together through the Transparency Crisis so far mostly by admitting
immediately that the Dream Archive audit was true.

## The regret (restricted)

At sixteen, Reza had the recurring dream — the one that comes before a
[gifting](../05-empowered-humans/fluxborn.md). A door in shallow water; a voice
asking if he'd hold it open. Being a sensible boy in an exam year, he ignored it,
and it stopped. He has read enough since to know exactly what he declined. He has
never told anyone, funds Fluxborn-integration programs with quiet intensity, and
still, some nights, stands in his washroom doorway like a man listening.

## Now

His succession is contested (Osei vs. Kalla); the classified-dreams question is a
live grenade in his desk; and he must decide before he leaves office whether to
declassify the withheld Dream Archive material — the one act that would define
his legacy and possibly detonate it.
