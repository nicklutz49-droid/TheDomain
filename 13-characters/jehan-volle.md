# Prosecutor Jehan Volle

Thirty-four; the youngest advocate ever raised to prosecutor before the Freeport
Yrshen Honor Court; the man building the case against the **Fog Understanding**
— and, though he doesn't know it yet, the case against his own history.

## The prosecutor

Orphaned at nine when pirates took the ferry *Gull's Errand* with his parents
aboard. Raised on his uncle's Life-Debt, cleared it by twenty-six through
scholarship bonds — a debt-system success story who believes in the system with
a convert's ferocity. His legal philosophy fits on a slate: *honor law admits no
arithmetic.* Grain tonnage does not offset treason. Full stop.

## The case

Eleven years of anomalies, patiently assembled: convoy routes that bend around
fog banks, Gray prizes that never include grain hulls, shrine-house witnesses
developing sudden vows of silence, and at the center, always,
[Admiral Kesari Ondo](kesari-ondo.md). The case is careful, honest, and nearly
complete. If it lands, it hangs an admiral, humiliates the
[Debt-Mother's](mahalia-wren.md) circle, and puts hunger back on the board as a
weapon in Confederacy waters. Volle has done the arithmetic on that last part.
He is *choosing* not to weigh it, because weighing it is the corruption he's
prosecuting.

## What he doesn't know (restricted)

The crew that took the *Gull's Errand* was one of the two crews
[Quartermaster Adaro](../09-power-and-conflict/the-gray-fleet.md) has **posted**
in eleven years — expelled from the Compact, their protected debts published to
the Honor Courts. The judgment that ruined his parents' killers was Gray Fleet
discipline, delivered under the very arrangement he is prosecuting. His justice
came from the outlaw ledger. The document trail that would show him is sitting
in his own evidence boxes, three folders from where he stopped reading.
