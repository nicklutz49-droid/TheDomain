# The Criminal Underworld

## Empire: Shadow Guilds

| Guild | Trade |
|---|---|
| The Coinless | theft |
| The Silence | assassination *(name collision with the [deadzone region](../07-geography/the-silence.md) — see [continuity notes](../00-meta/continuity-notes.md))* |
| The Smokehouse | drugs |
| The Flesh Trade | trafficking |

## Union: merit manipulation

- Exam fraud (attacking the [Great Exam](../08-civilization/education.md))
- Merit inflation
- The **Freedom Network** — helping citizens escape surveillance

## Confederacy: debt crime

- Debt forgery
- Piracy — the **Gray Fleet**
- Mask black market (upstream: the [Mask-Weavers](secret-societies/the-mask-weavers.md))

## Cross-power

- **Silk Passage Syndicate** — smuggling, protection, information brokerage along the [Silk Passage](../07-geography/neutral-territories.md).
- **Relic Traffickers** — pre-collapse artifacts and forbidden knowledge (prime source: [the Sunken Halls](../06-threats-and-anomalies/glitch-zones/the-sunken-halls.md)).
- **Fluxborn for Hire** — independent [Fluxborn](../05-empowered-humans/fluxborn.md) operating outside legitimate channels.

## Open threads

- Each power's crime mirrors its virtue: the Empire's hierarchy breeds guilds, the Union's transparency breeds privacy crime, the Confederacy's debts breed forgery. New crimes should keep that symmetry.
- Relic traffickers are the most likely accidental triggers of [hidden-truth](../03-hidden-truth/_index.md) reveals — every fenced artifact is a plot grenade.
- The Freedom Network is framed as crime but reads as dissidence — is it criminal, political, or the Union's own deniable safety valve?
