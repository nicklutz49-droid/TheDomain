# Military and Warfare

War is brutal, with divine champions clashing alongside conscripts. The real threat
isn't always the enemy army — it's what manifests when too much blood soaks
contested ground.

## Empire military

- **Imperial Legions** — professional armies. A Standard Legion (5,000): heavy infantry, light infantry, cavalry, Architect-Engineers, Flux-Artillery. Over **800 combat airships**.
- **Knightly Orders** — elite units answering to patron gods:
  - **Order of the Crimson Dawn** — berserkers ([Vaelthrix](../04-divine-beings/the-twelve/vaelthrix.md))
  - **Order of the Mountain's Root** — siege specialists ([Mordrath](../04-divine-beings/the-twelve/mordrath.md))
  - **Order of the Veiled Throne** — intelligence/assassination ([Thessaly](../04-divine-beings/the-twelve/thessaly.md))
- **Doctrine:** overwhelming force, disciplined formations, willingness to accept catastrophic casualties.

## Union military

- **Collective Defense Force** — no standing armies. Every citizen trained. Regional cohorts mobilize as needed. Tier 3 (Federal) mobilization: **2+ million**.
- **Architect Corps** — the Union's true advantage: **60% of the world's trained Architects**. ~400 combat airships with superior Architect integration.
- **Doctrine:** intelligence, precision, minimizing casualties.

## Confederacy military

- **Free Fleets** — naval and airship power. Combined sea–air operations standard.
- **The Thousand Blades** — mercenary companies within the Confederacy. Fighting for outsiders is death.
- **Doctrine:** fluid, opportunistic, unpredictable. Avoid pitched battles.

## Warfare complications

- **Hollow manifestation** — battlefields with 10,000+ deaths in a week have a **40% chance** of developing permanent glitch activity.
- **Divine intervention rules** — gods may bless troops and empower Fluxborn. Direct combat, destroying infrastructure, or killing enemy leadership is prohibited (see [the Twelve's Rules](../04-divine-beings/nature-of-divinity.md)).
- **The Fluxborn Accords** — captured Fluxborn are ransomed, not executed. Honored in spirit, violated in practice.

## Open threads

- The 40% rule makes attrition warfare literally self-defeating — every general knows a big enough victory poisons the ground won. Doctrine should be shaped around this; only the Empire's casualty-tolerance seems to ignore it.
