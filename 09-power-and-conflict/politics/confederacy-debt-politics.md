# Confederacy Debt Politics

The [Confederacy](../../07-geography/confederacy.md) has no throne and no assembly.
It has the **Great Ledger** — the informal, universally understood reckoning of who
owes whom, kept in ten thousand shrine-books and one old woman's head.

## The Debt-Mother

[Mahalia Wren](../../13-characters/mahalia-wren.md) of Freeport Yrshen is the most
*owed* person alive — favor-webs running through three admiralties, forty guilds,
and (it is said) the harbor stones themselves. She rules nothing and refuses
nothing is refused to her. Her genius is that she **never calls debts due**: the
threat is the power, spent debt is dead debt.

## Live disputes

- **Storm-tithes:** [Korrath's](../../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/korrath.md) mountain league invoices the lowlands for the weather his peaks feed into [Selvane's](../../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/selvane.md) rivers. The lowlands dispute the theology *and* the arithmetic. Two gods, one watershed, no precedent.
- **The Fog Understanding:** Admiral [Kesari Ondo's](../../13-characters/kesari-ondo.md) unwritten arrangement with the Gray Fleet (pirates spare grain ships; the Free Fleets don't hunt in fog) keeps people fed and is, formally, treason. Half the Honor Courts know. The debt logic is airtight; the honor logic is fatal; the Confederacy is enjoying the paradox.
- **The mask census:** Spice Gate proposes registering mask-identities for contract enforcement. The [Mask-Weavers](../secret-societies/the-mask-weavers.md) responded with one sentence: *"A counted face is a taken face."* The proposal is dying, but the fact it was utterable at all marks a generational shift.

## How anything gets decided

Debt-conclaves: the relevant creditors sit, recite obligations, and the person the
room owes most speaks last. Outsiders call it oligarchy by bookkeeping. It is — and
it has survived every crisis the tidy powers didn't.

## Open threads

- Wren is in her seventies with no designated heir to the favor-web. Debt disperses on death (see [Death & Afterlife](../../08-civilization/death-and-afterlife.md)) — unless she binds it to someone first. Every faction in the Confederacy is being kind to her at once, which she finds hilarious.
- Her private ledger of debts *she* owes is one page. The last entry reads only **"Y."**
