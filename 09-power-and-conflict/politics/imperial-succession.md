# The Imperial Succession

[Emperor Aurelian IX](../../13-characters/emperor-aurelian-ix.md) is 71, forty
years on the throne, and failing faster than the court admits. Three heirs, four
gods, five Great Houses — the Empire's next decade is being decided now.

## The heirs

- **Crown Prince Darion**, 48 — soldier, Wound-hawk, war-college orthodox. Backed by [House Valdric](../../07-geography/feudal-empire/great-houses.md) and favored by [Vaelthrix's](../../04-divine-beings/the-twelve/vaelthrix.md) orders. Platform: the Wound is expanding and the Empire must militarize the frontier "before the frontier militarizes us." Uses [Kravenhold's](../../13-characters/roswitha-kravenhold.md) expansion reports — the edited versions.
- **[Archduchess Elara](../../13-characters/archduchess-elara.md)**, 44 — administrator, reformer, the clever one. Backed by [House Thessian](../../07-geography/feudal-empire/great-houses.md); [Thessaly](../../04-divine-beings/the-twelve/thessaly.md) finds her *interesting*, which frightens Elara's own staff. Platform: the Empire is losing the cold war economically; modernize the guilds, settle Lowgate, tax waste as heresy-in-practice. *(Full file — including her tutor's history — at [13-characters/archduchess-elara.md](../../13-characters/archduchess-elara.md).)*
- **Prince Lucan**, 19 — the late child. Charming, debt-entangled, and conspicuously sponsored by [Syreth's](../../04-divine-beings/the-twelve/syreth.md) temple-banks, which have quietly bought his household's obligations. Platform: none yet. That's what worries people.

## The kingmakers

- **The Houses:** Valdric (Darion), Thessian (Elara), Merentis (waiting for a naval concession), Oranthos (selling mining futures to both camps), Kravenhold (silent — the frontier can't afford politics).
- **The gods:** Vaelthrix wants the soldier; Thessaly professes neutrality while running the board; Syreth hedges all three; [Mordrath](../../04-divine-beings/the-twelve/mordrath.md) has said one sentence in forty years of privy council — *"Choose the one who repairs."* No one agrees what it means.

## The complication (restricted)

The Emperor petitioned [Kythera](../../04-divine-beings/the-twelve/kythera.md) to
mend his failing heart. **She refused** — gently, without explanation. The privy
council has suppressed this; the theological implications of the Mending Hand
declining an Emperor are worse than the medical ones.
