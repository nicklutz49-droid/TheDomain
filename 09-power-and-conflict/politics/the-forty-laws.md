# The Forty Laws

Schedule One of the Union Charter: **the Surrendered Laws**. At the founding
conference, [Ibrahim the Knot](../../13-characters/ibrahim-the-knot.md) required
every signatory nation to surrender one *treasured* law into the shared charter —
giving up sole ownership so that the law became everyone's. Each law still bears
its donor's name, and reciting "the Forty" is how Union schoolchildren learn the
federation's shape: each law is a nation's character in one line.

Twenty are written below; the remaining twenty are open slots — **add a law, add
a nation.**

| # | Law | Donor nation | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | No one may be tried in a language they do not dream in. | Tel Roshan | The origin of the Union's court-interpreter corps — and, lately, an awkward question: who verifies the dreaming? |
| 2 | Water debts are repaid in water. | Qafara | Desert law: you cannot buy your way out of a well you drained. |
| 3 | A teacher may not be conscripted. | Amsara | Even at Tier 3 mobilization. Tested twice; held twice. |
| 4 | Every third year, the record must be read aloud. | Meridal | Ancestor of the [Lantern Audit](../../08-civilization/festivals-and-holidays.md). |
| 5 | No door may be locked against a census-taker. | Veshret | The Transparency Crisis's favorite law to quote, on both sides. |
| 6 | The dead may not be named in contracts. | Iskel | Debt dies with the debtor — the Union's quiet rebuke to Confederacy practice. |
| 7 | Grain may not be collateral. | Green Amsara | You may lend against gold, never against bread. |
| 8 | A confession heard while healing is heard by no one. | Kytheral | Medical privilege, absolute — even the Merit Courts cannot subpoena a healer. |
| 9 | No law may forbid leaving. | Sarn March | The border nation's gift; the legal root of every exile-instead-of-prison sentence — and the Freedom Network's favorite defense. |
| 10 | An accusation written anonymously weighs nothing. | Old Persis | Merit-society hygiene: the state may watch, but neighbors may not whisper. |
| 11 | The river's name may not be sold. | Two Banks | No private ownership of waters, harbors, or their naming. |
| 12 | A child's Exam may be retaken once, by right, without cause given. | Little Wadi | The smallest nation's law and the most beloved: everyone knows someone the second chance saved. |
| 13 | No official may profit from a rule they wrote. | Verantum District | Enforced by the Kept Word's notaries with genuine relish. |
| 14 | The hungry stranger eats first and is asked after. | Green Heart | [Anverra's](../../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/anverra.md) law before it was anyone's. |
| 15 | No one may be denied shade or water at a public well. | The Erg Nations | The desert's minimum decency, now enforced in rainy provinces that have never needed it and keep it anyway. |
| 16 | The census may count, but never name. | Dar Essa | Sits in permanent productive tension with law 5 — the two nations' delegates have argued it for thirteen centuries, ceremonially, at every charter anniversary. |
| 17 | Every school must teach the losing argument. | Qum Sarat | The reason Union rhetoric training is feared in three powers' courts. |
| 18 | No debt survives the deep desert. | The Sand Reaches | Cross the waste and arrive clean. The Freedom Network's southern route exists because of it; so does a small industry of people who don't survive the crossing. |
| 19 | A soldier's vote weighs the same as a general's. | Sahel Verge | The Collective Defense Force's civilian soul in one line. |
| 20 | No one owns the sky's light. | High Atlas | Written for sun and stars; now the governing law in every lumen-farming dispute, which its authors could not have imagined. |

## The unwritten twenty

Laws 21–40 are deliberately open. Conventions for adding one: name the donor
nation (inventing it is the point — each law implies its nation), keep it to one
sentence, and give it one note showing where it rubs against modern life. The
friction is the story.
