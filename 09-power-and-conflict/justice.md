# Crime, Justice, and Punishment

## Imperial justice

Crimes classified by **who committed them against whom**. Punishments: debt-binding,
scarring, forced labor, execution, and **"unmanning"** — erasing name and legal
existence. Nobles are tried only by peers.

## Union justice

**Merit Court System.** Emphasis on rehabilitation. Sentences: civic service,
re-education, exile, termination (rare).

## Confederacy justice

**Honor Court.** Crime creates debt. Resolution: payment, service bond, trial by
combat, mask-stripping, drowning. Murder creates permanent **Blood-Debt** on the
criminal's family.
