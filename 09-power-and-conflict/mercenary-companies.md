# Mercenary Companies

## Major companies

| Company | Base | Profile |
|---|---|---|
| **The Iron Covenant** | Empire | 3,000 veterans. Defensive specialists. **Never broken a contract.** |
| **The Shifting Company** | Union | 200 Architects plus support. Sophisticated Flux-warfare. |
| **The Thousand Masks** | Confederacy | 500–2,000 specialists in sabotage and assassination. |
| **The Pilgrims** | unaffiliated | ~300 glitch zone specialists. Relay repair, artifact recovery. |

*(The Confederacy's internal mercenary ecosystem — the Thousand Blades — is
described under [Military](military.md); fighting for outsiders is death.)*

## Notable free agents

- **"Grandmother" Chen** — deadliest assassin alive. Rumored ability: *"being underestimated."*
- **Laughing Marcus** — Fire [Fluxborn](../05-empowered-humans/fluxborn.md) who fights for entertainment.
- **The Sisters Three** — Light, Shadow, and unknown-affinity Fluxborn operating together.

## Open threads

- The Pilgrims do relay repair for pay — the same work as sacred [glitch zone pilgrimage](../10-supplementary/pilgrimage.md). Blessed professionals or blasphemous profiteers, depending on who's asked.
- "Being underestimated" as a literal Flux ability is either a joke or the most terrifying power in the setting — a canon ruling would be fun either way.
- The Sisters Three's third affinity is deliberately unknown — as is whether they're actually sisters.
