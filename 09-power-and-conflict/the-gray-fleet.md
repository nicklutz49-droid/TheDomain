# The Gray Fleet

The Confederacy's pirates — promoted from a line in the
[criminal underworld](criminal-underworld.md) to their own file, because the other
side of the [Fog Understanding](../13-characters/kesari-ondo.md) deserved someone
standing on it.

## What it actually is

Not a navy and not a mob: a drifting confederation of forty-odd crews bound by the
**Gray Compact** — three rules recited at every muster: *spare the ship, share the
water, never sell a crewmate's debt.* Most Gray hands are **debt-outlaws**: people
whose obligations compounded past any honest horizon — hereditary Life-Debt gone
malignant, Blood-Debt inherited from a murderer father, ledger-ruin. The Fleet is
where the Confederacy's own arithmetic sends the people it breaks. Everyone
ashore knows this, which is why the piracy is prosecuted and the pirates,
individually, so rarely are.

## Faith

Devout congregants of [Maru](../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/maru.md) — a tithe
from every prize, thrown from the stern. The Fleet's departure-prayer is the
ordinary one with one line added: *"and a wake behind us wide enough to lose our
names in."*

## Quartermaster Sefa Adaro

The Understanding's keeper on the Gray side: quartermaster of the *Long Odds*,
sixty, ledger-exact, a debt-outlaw of the old kind (she inherited a Blood-Debt at
nine years old for a killing done before her birth). It was Adaro who met
[Admiral Ondo](../13-characters/kesari-ondo.md) in the shrine-house eleven years
ago, and Adaro who has held forty crews to one unwritten sentence since — by the
only discipline the Fleet respects: any crew that touches a grain ship is
**posted** — its debts, the ones the Compact swore never to sell, published to
every Honor Court on the coast. She has posted two crews in eleven years. There
has not been a third.

## Open threads

- Adaro keeps a second book: the names of every child aboard the Fleet born into inherited debt. What she intends to do with it — and with the leverage of the Understanding — is the Fleet's real long game.
- If the Honor Court prosecution exposes the Understanding, Adaro's posted-debts weapon works in reverse: she can prove *who ashore knew*. Mutually assured bookkeeping.
- The Compact's third rule has one loophole no one has tested: a crewmate's debt can't be *sold* — but it can be forgiven. Who has that kind of money? [The Debt-Mother](../13-characters/mahalia-wren.md) has noticed the Fleet exists. The Fleet has noticed her noticing.
