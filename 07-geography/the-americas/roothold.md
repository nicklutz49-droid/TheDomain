# Roothold

[Grandmother Cedar's](../../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/grandmother-cedar.md)
confederation of forest nations in the rain-green northwest — the gentlest absolute
rule on Earth.

## How it works

The capital is a living forest grown to need: bridges thicken under traffic, halls
bloom for festivals, homes are *asked for* rather than built. Law is made in
**consensus groves** — disputes argued until the canopy is said to lean — and
enforced by the only sentence that matters: exile from the green. Felling a living
tree without leave is the unforgivable crime; the last person to do it is a
cautionary tale three centuries old.

## Life there

Slow by eastern standards and content about it. Orchard-tending as civic duty,
memory-keeping as profession (the confederation's history is oral, redundant, and
startlingly accurate), and a deep, practiced patience with the god who is also the
landscape. Children learn early: *Grandmother forgives twice.*

## Foreign relations

One trade road crosses her forests to the [Free Coasts](the-free-coasts.md),
audited by the god personally. The [Mirror Throne](mirror-throne.md) border is
quiet in the way held breath is quiet. Eastern visitors describe Roothold as
paradise for exactly as long as they don't test rule three.

- Exiles of Roothold carry seeds they're forbidden to plant. Some end up on the Free Coasts. What grows if one breaks the rule?
