# The Free Coasts

A league of harbor city-states along the eastern seaboard — the hinge between the
Americas and the three powers, and the only major polity on Earth that is
**godless by charter**: its founding compact bars any god from rule, residency, or
shrine within the harbor walls.

## How it works

Sixteen chartered ports, each governed by a **Harbor Table** of shipowners,
factors, and (voting last, by custom) one elected debtor "to keep the rich
honest." League-wide decisions need nine harbors; league-wide wars need sixteen,
which is the point.

## Why it matters

- **The only door.** Nearly all trade between the powers and the god-ruled interior clears the Free Coasts. Mirror-glass, Roothold timber-craft, and interior curiosities go east; Flux devices, textiles, and [Syreth's](../../04-divine-beings/the-twelve/syreth.md) credit go west.
- **The listening post.** Every intelligence service keeps a factor's office here. The Coasts tolerate all of them on the ancient principle that spies pay double for everything.
- **The loophole.** Gods can't *rule* here — but Syreth's temple-banks hold paper on half the Tables. The charter's authors barred thrones and forgot ledgers.

## Life there

Loud, polyglot, contractual. The godless charter breeds a distinct civic pride —
shrines cluster *outside* the walls in a belt called the Kneeling Mile, so the
devout commute to pray. Free Coast idiom for something reliable: "honest as a wall."

## Open threads

- If the Far Accord exists, the Coasts are where east and west actually touch — is the league the Accord's designated valve, or its blind spot?
- A Harbor Table discovering how much of its debt Syreth quietly holds is a ready-made political thriller: the god-free republic that mortgaged itself to a god.
