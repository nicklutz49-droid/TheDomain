# The Mirror Throne

[Tezcamotl's](../../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/tezcamotl.md) realm: terraced
mountain cities on the Inland Sea's western shore, black-glass architecture, and
the most complete surveillance society on Earth — run not by bureaucracy but by
theology.

## How it works

Every subject carries a **mirror-shard**; through them the Obsidian Mirror sees his
realm continuously. Tribute is **mirror-tithe**: hours spent before the great
Mirror in the capital, being thoroughly *seen* — confession, census, and communion
in one act. Subjects are taught that to be seen is to be loved; crime is rare,
privacy is a foreign vice, and the word for "alone" is the word for "lost."

## Life there

Orderly, prosperous, humming with low dread the inhabitants would not call dread.
Festivals of reflection; artisan guilds producing mirror-work the Free Coasts pay
fortunes for; a standing priesthood of **Facers** who interpret what the Mirror
wants. The border is one-way for most: leaving means taking a shard, and taking a
shard means never quite having left.

## Foreign relations

Trades obsidian, silver, and scrying-glass through the
[Free Coasts](the-free-coasts.md); regards the three powers as "the unwatched
lands" — pitied, not feared. The quiet border with [Roothold](roothold.md) is the
continent's philosophical fault line: total sight versus total patience.
