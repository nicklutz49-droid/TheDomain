# The Pale Interior

Life inside the [Pale Sovereignty](pale-sovereignty.md), assembled from the only
two outside sources that exist: the **Kite Families'** records of the final
ordinary week (200 years old) and the testimony of
[Ashka](../13-characters/ashka-of-the-pale.md) (2 years old). Everything here is
intelligence, not common knowledge — and two centuries of drift sit between the
sources.

## The Bright Terraces

The Pale's cities are **grown**: terraced hive-structures of pale organic
material, warm to the touch, self-repairing, rearranging slowly to the
[Radiance's](#the-radiance) design. Streets are different in the morning.
Lighting is bioluminescent and low — hence the population's dark-adapted eyes.
There is no ruin anywhere: damage heals. Ashka's flat observation: *"Nothing in
the Pale is ever broken. You learn to miss broken things."*

## The castes

- **Relay-singers** — the technical clergy: they coax the grown-technology by tone and cadence. Ashka's caste. Increasingly reassigned coastward (see below).
- **Root-farmers** — tend the food-growth. The largest caste, the least watched.
- **Wardens of the Veil** — the border clergy who tend the Maze's inner edge and administer the doctrine of the outside.
- **The Attuned** — new since the Kite records: those who have served at the drowned coast and come back *changed*. Serene, tireless, singing in their sleep in no known language. They are honored, resettled together, and visited by everyone and befriended by no one.

## The Radiance

Palladia's church, which is to say Palladia's state. The God-Queen herself is
rarely seen — a white light over the sea on certain nights, a voice in the
grown walls on high liturgies. Doctrine holds that the outside world **fell** —
the Drowned Lands — and that the Pale is the ark that was saved. The Maze is not
a prison in Pale teaching. It is the hull.

## The Descending Liturgy

Daily, at first-light, every congregation recites **the Number**, and the Number
goes down by one each day. No doctrine explains it; it is treated as the holiest
thing there is. The Number was **2,922** the morning Ashka left. It stands near
**2,190** now — a countdown reaching zero roughly **six years from the present**
(~2,102 AC): *two years after the
[Centenary Conclave](../09-power-and-conflict/politics/divine-politics.md).*

## The drowned coast

The sunken relay-temple Palladia went to find the week the fog came
([Pale Closure](../01-foundation/events/the-pale-closure.md)) is now the Pale's
great work: relay-singer rotations, growing numbers, no returning descriptions —
those who serve there return Attuned or not at all. Whatever is being sung down
there has been in progress for decades and is presumably what the Number counts
toward.
