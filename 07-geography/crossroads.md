# Crossroads

The city where three roads and three powers meet — and where, twenty-one
centuries ago, thirteen seats were set on the summit ground and one stayed
empty. The most consequential neutral ground on Earth, now four years from
hosting the [22nd Centenary Conclave](../09-power-and-conflict/politics/divine-politics.md).

## What it is

The [Concordance's](../01-foundation/events/the-concordance.md) camp never fully
dispersed; Crossroads is what three thousand years of not-dispersing looks like.
A caravan city of stone khans and lamplit arcades on the
[Silk Passage](neutral-territories.md), it has been sacked by no one, claimed by
everyone, and successfully governed by, of all things, innkeepers.

## Governance

**The Road Court**: a council of the hostelers' guild, the pilgrim-guides'
lodge, the caravan insurers — and, by a 300-year-old arrangement nobody defends
aloud and nobody touches, one seat held by the
[Silk Passage Syndicate](../09-power-and-conflict/criminal-underworld.md). The
Court's one law above all others: *the roads stay open.* Its enforcement arm is
economic exile — a merchant the Road Court posts cannot buy water from here to
Fort Perseverance.

## Sacred ground

- **The Summit Ground** — the Concordance site: thirteen seats, re-set by tradition each year. On [Concordance Day](../08-civilization/festivals-and-holidays.md), thirteen lamps; the thirteenth lit last.
- **[Breccia's](../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/breccia.md) shrine** — the city's true patron; [Saint Verilee's staff](../05-empowered-humans/legendary-figures/saint-verilee.md) rests here, touched by every departing Grand Pilgrim.
- **The Armistice Stone** — where the [Shatter War](../01-foundation/events/the-shatter-war.md) ended, kept polished by the veterans' lodges of *both* signatory powers on alternating months.

## The present moment

The Conclave is coming, and Crossroads is quietly becoming the most-watched
small city in the world: every intelligence service refreshing its safehouses,
the Road Court pricing the century's lodging contracts, the Syndicate selling
the same rooftops to three buyers. Somewhere in the middle of it,
[Ashka of the Pale](../13-characters/ashka-of-the-pale.md) is hiding in a city
that professionally does not ask names — under the informal protection of a
goddess who notices who walks her roads gently.

## Open threads

- Who re-sets the thirteen seats each year? The hostelers say the pilgrim-guides; the guides say the temple; the temple says the seats are already set when they arrive.
- Four years out, Conclave logistics are a story engine: accrediting divine retinues, feeding delegations from three powers, and keeping the Pale Watch's rumor mill out of the summit ground.
