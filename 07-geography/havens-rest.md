# Haven's Rest

The city of neutral rooms. Where [Crossroads](crossroads.md) is where the powers
meet, Haven's Rest is where they *agree* — the [Fluxborn Accords](../01-foundation/events/battle-of-red-sands.md)
were signed here ~740 years ago, and every treaty since has wanted the same
address. Local proverb, carved over the east gate: **"What rests at Haven's
rests."**

## What it is

The middle city of the [Silk Passage](neutral-territories.md), a day's airship
haul from Crossroads and three from [Fort Perseverance](fort-perseverance.md).
It grew up around a caravanserai with an unusual reputation: fights started
inside its walls stayed unfinished, because the hosteler would feed neither
party until they talked. Seven centuries of formalizing that instinct produced
a city whose chief export is *concluded arguments*.

## The Neutral Rooms

**The Neutral Rooms** are chartered parley-chambers — some seat two, one seats
four hundred — rented from the **Roomkeepers' Guild** with a guild-backed
guarantee of safe-conduct: in, through, and three days out. No weapons pass the
threshold; no Echo-Birds; a Roomkeeper stands witness and says nothing, ever.

Enforcement is the Passage's own family of law, the same lineage as the
Crossroads [Road Court](crossroads.md): not soldiers but **hospitality
withdrawal**. Break a Room's peace and every hostel, well, and stable from
Crossroads to Fort Perseverance closes to you *and yours* — house, firm, and
descendants — for a generation. It has been invoked four times in seven hundred
years. All four names are still taught to children as curses.

## Institutions

- **The pilgrim hospitals** — largest on the [pilgrimage roads](../10-supplementary/pilgrimage.md); healing matrices run in public wards regardless of power, coin, or mask. Convalescents who die here are logged as "resting," a courtesy to [Morrow](../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/morrow.md), whose small shrine stands at the hospital gate.
- **The Treaty Archive** — a certified copy of every accord signed in the Rooms, including the [Shatter War](../01-foundation/events/the-shatter-war.md) settlement instruments (the armistice itself was signed at [Crossroads](crossroads.md); the year of ratifications that made it stick was negotiated here, room by room) and the original Accords sheet bearing [Sahar of the Glass Banner's](../05-empowered-humans/legendary-figures/sahar-of-the-glass-banner.md) signature. Consultation is open; *interpretation* costs extra.
- **The Amaranthine hospice** — outside the walls, where [Sister Amaranth](../13-characters/sister-amaranth.md), the Backward Saint, is kept by her Watch. The city treats her as its strange patron: she has already attended every future treaty, after all. Archive scribes joke that Haven's Rest has two industries, agreements and prophecy, and the filing rule is to keep them separate — *(restricted: the Watch's recent failure to forward her statements is quietly straining that rule).*

## Economy

Neutral banking — the only counting-houses that exchange Imperial Marks, Merit
Credit, and Honor Bond paper across one desk (see
[Economics](../08-civilization/economics.md)), with Flux Shards as the honest
denominator. Beyond that: convalescence for the Passage's wealthy sick, and the
mediation trade — professional seconds, oath-scriveners, insult-appraisers, and
the Roomkeepers themselves, whose silence commands the highest fee of all. The
[Syndicate](../09-power-and-conflict/criminal-underworld.md) keeps a famously
light hand here; even smugglers need somewhere to arbitrate.

## The present moment

The [22nd Centenary Conclave](../09-power-and-conflict/politics/divine-politics.md)
is four years out at Crossroads — which means every power wants its
*pre*-Conclave positioning settled here first. The Rooms are booked solid
through all four years; the Roomkeepers' waitlist is now itself intelligence
worth killing for, which is exactly the kind of sentence that gets a city's
peace tested. The Guild has begun training witnesses at double rate and will
not say why.

- The Treaty Archive holds a sealed annex older than the Accords. The Roomkeepers pay its upkeep without opening it. Standing arrangement — or standing instruction?
- If the city's patron saint has "already attended" the pre-Conclave meetings, several delegations have quietly asked the Watch for transcripts. What happens when one delegation doesn't ask?
