# The Pale Sovereignty

**Location:** Former China and Korea, around the Yellow Sea

For two centuries, the Pale has been sealed behind
[a maze of impenetrable glitch zones](../06-threats-and-anomalies/glitch-zones/the-pale-maze.md)
(the Pale Closure, ~200 ya). The population inside has developed in isolation under
the absolute rule of a god-queen.

## The God-Queen: Palladia the Eternal

A [minor god](../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/palladia.md) trapped when the maze
formed. Over two centuries she has transformed into something no longer truly
humanoid — a towering figure of white light and crystalline structure. She has
achieved **power rivaling the Twelve**. She may know
[the truth about divine nature](../04-divine-beings/nature-of-divinity.md).

## Pale society

- **Population:** estimated 5–8 million.
- **Physical changes:** extremely pale skin, eyes adapted to low light, unusual height.
- **Technology:** organic technology that *grows* rather than is built. Buildings sprout from the ground; tools heal when damaged.
- **Culture:** absolute theocracy. The outside world is taught as myth.

## Threat assessment

If the barriers fell, conflict between Palladia and the Twelve could devastate
civilizations.

## Open threads

- Two centuries is ~8 human generations of divergence — language drift (does the universal translator still work through the maze?), doctrine, and biology.
- Canonical narrative seed: someone escapes the Pale with knowledge of what Palladia is planning.
