# Fort Perseverance

The [Silk Passage's](neutral-territories.md) shield. A walled caravan-fortress
at the road's northern elbow, where the short way between
[Crossroads](crossroads.md) and [Haven's Rest](havens-rest.md) bends within
sight of the [Northern Wastes](../06-threats-and-anomalies/glitch-zones/northern-wastes.md) —
the largest glitch region on Earth, total corruption, impassable. The Fort
exists so the road can pass *close* to the short way without dying on it.

## What it is

Every other city on the Passage grew around trade. Fort Perseverance grew
around a wall, and the trade came because the wall held. Its khans are inside
casemates; its [Lumen-Tree](../10-supplementary/ecology.md) avenues run
straight so horns carry; its [rail spur](../08-civilization/technology/rails.md)
and [airship](../08-civilization/technology/airships.md) ground both terminate
*inside* the fortifications. Nothing here pretends the north isn't there.

## The subscription garrison

The Fort flies no flag. Its walls are manned by the **Toll Companies** —
professional wall-troops funded by a per-caravan levy collected at both gates,
paid without complaint in Imperial Marks, Merit Credit, and Honor Bond scrip
alike. It is the only place on Earth where all three powers' money jointly
funds soldiers. Diplomats cite it constantly, in two flavors: proof that
cooperation is possible, or proof of exactly what it takes to buy it — a
nightmare on the horizon and no other road.

Company charters are held by the gate-reeves' council, which answers, loosely,
to the Road Court in [Crossroads](crossroads.md). Loosely. The Court's writ of
economic exile famously runs "from here to Fort Perseverance" — the Fort is
where the road's law ends and the wall's law begins.

## The Watch

The Fort's early warning is alive and on a leash. Kennels of
**[Compass-Hounds](../10-supplementary/ecology.md)** are walked the wall
circuit day and night; the hounds' inner sense always points to the nearest
[relay](../02-the-domain/infrastructure.md), and when a hound loses the scent — circling,
whining, pointing nowhere — the field is failing and the horns sound. Three
hounds blind at once empties the outer ward.

Lamp-lines and watch-fires back the kennels: chains of lights from wall to
citadel that must stay visibly lit, because a lamp that gutters for no reason
is itself a report. Evacuation drills run every **hand** — the local word for
the [six-day week](../08-civilization/calendar.md), five workdays and a
Templeday — and every household, caravanserai, and company mess counts heads
at the drill-horn. Everyone counts heads, always. A Fort child learns their
muster point before their letters.

## Wastes-runners

The Wastes are impassable. They are also the largest untouched pre-collapse
salvage field in existence, and so **Wastes-runners** — illegal crews slipping
north past the picket lines for relics — die generation after generation
trying to be the exception. The Fort's policy is coherent only from inside:
it hangs runners when it catches them going out, and buys from them when it
doesn't. Every gate-reeve knows which assessors quietly grade Wastes salvage
for the [relic traffickers](../09-power-and-conflict/criminal-underworld.md),
and the gallows outside the north gate stands in sight of the shop that pays
best. Nobody calls this hypocrisy. The rope is for leading the Wastes *back
to the wall*; the coin is for what you carried if you didn't.

## Culture

Grim, practical, proud. The Fort's toast is **"Walls hold."** — said flat,
glasses down, no clink. Waste of any kind offends even non-Imperials here;
the wall has made everyone a rationer. Shrines to
[Breccia](../04-divine-beings/minor-gods/breccia.md) stand at both gates,
facing along the road rather than at each other.

The [Pilgrims](../09-power-and-conflict/mercenary-companies.md) mercenary
company winters at the Fort by long custom, drilling beside the Toll
Companies they refuse to join — wintering here is respectable; taking the
subscription coin, to a Pilgrim, is retirement.

## Open threads

- What have the Toll Companies actually repelled? The horns have sounded in earnest within living memory — the veterans won't describe what the hounds went blind at.
- The per-caravan levy is the world's only three-power budget. Who audits it, and what happens the first time a power withholds its caravans to starve the wall?
- Somewhere in the Fort is a standing price list for Wastes salvage, updated in chalk. Who sets the prices, and why did "unbroken mirrors" triple last year?
- Wastes-runners swear the corruption's southern edge is not where the maps put it. The gate-reeves have stopped writing their reports down.
